## Deep Investigation: The Ōhanga Māori Framework

### Core Concept: Economics as Relationship Management
*(Not just production/distribution, but maintaining balance between people, environment, and spiritual realms)*

### Phase 1: Foundations - The Reciprocal Economy
**Activity:** "Whakapapa of Resources" Mapping  
- Students trace the journey of key resources (pounamu, obsidian, preserved birds, woven cloaks)
- Map the relationships between: resource → skills → trade routes → cultural value
- **Depth Focus:** Understanding how value was created through skill, scarcity, and spiritual significance

### Phase 2: Systems Thinking - The Interconnected Web
**Activity:** "Economic Whakapapa" Network Analysis  
- Create visual webs showing connections between: mahinga kai (food gathering), tohunga knowledge, tribal relationships, seasonal cycles
- **Case Study:** Analyze a specific item's economic journey (e.g., a pounamu mere)
- **Critical Question:** "How did economic activity reinforce social structure and environmental sustainability simultaneously?"

### Phase 3: Comparative Depth - Western vs Māori Economic Paradigms
**Analysis Framework:**
| Aspect | Western Capitalist Model | Ōhanga Māori Model |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| **Wealth Definition** | Accumulation of capital | Maintenance of relationships |
| **Resource View** | Commodities to exploit | Taonga to nurture |
| **Success Measure** | Profit/growth | Community wellbeing |
| **Time Orientation** | Short-term gains | Multi-generational thinking |

## Cultural Wisdom Connections

### Tikanga Economic Principles:
- **Utu:** Reciprocal exchange (not merely transactional)
- **Mana:** Enhancing prestige through generosity rather than accumulation
- **Tapu:** Spiritual restrictions regulating resource use
- **Rahui:** Temporary bans ensuring sustainability

**Deep Inquiry:** "How did spiritual beliefs actually create economic stability and sustainability?"

**Mātauranga Anchor:** The Maramataka (Māori lunar calendar) as an economic planning tool - aligning economic activity with natural cycles and ecological knowledge.
