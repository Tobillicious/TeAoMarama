## Rich Text Resource: Aotearoa's Iconic Ecosystems

### 🌲 **Kauri Forest Ecosystem Data**

**Waipoua Forest Sanctuary - Northern Kauri Ecosystem**

- **Canopy Height**: 30-50 meters (mature kauri)
- **Age Range**: 200-2000+ years for mature trees
- **Biodiversity**: 600+ native plant species, 40+ bird species
- **Endemic Species**: 85% of plants found nowhere else on Earth

**Key Species Relationships**:

- **Kauri Trees** (Agathis australis): Forest giants, ecosystem engineers
- **Kererū** (Native pigeon): Key seed disperser for large-seeded plants
- **Tui**: Pollinator for native flowers, nectar feeder
- **Wētā**: Decomposer, prey species for birds and spiders
- **Native fungi**: Mycorrhizal partners with tree roots

### 🌊 **Coastal Wetland Ecosystem**

**Firth of Thames Wetland System**

- **Water salinity**: Ranges from 0.5% (fresh) to 3.5% (seawater)
- **Tidal range**: 3.2 meters at spring tide
- **Primary productivity**: 2000g/m²/year (carbon fixation)
- **Migratory birds**: 40,000+ arrive annually from Arctic

**Ecosystem Services Provided**:

- **Flood protection**: Absorbs 70% of storm surge energy
- **Water filtration**: Removes 85% of nitrogen pollution
- **Carbon storage**: 3x more carbon per hectare than forests
- **Nursery habitat**: 60% of coastal fish species depend on wetlands

### 🏔️ **Alpine Ecosystem - Tongariro National Park**

**Environmental Data**:

- **Altitude range**: 1000-2797 meters above sea level
- **Temperature range**: -10°C to 25°C annually
- **Growing season**: 4-6 months above treeline
- **UV radiation**: 40% higher than sea level

## Three Ways to Use This Resource

### 🔗 **Activity 1: Food Web Construction**

**Time**: 30 minutes | **Materials**: Ecosystem cards, string, chart paper

**Task**: Build a food web for a kauri forest ecosystem

1. **Identify producers**: Kauri trees, rimu, tree ferns, mosses
2. **Primary consumers**: Kererū, insects, wētā
3. **Secondary consumers**: Tui, fantail, gecko
4. **Tertiary consumers**: Morepork, falcon
5. **Decomposers**: Fungi, bacteria, invertebrates

**Analysis Questions**:

- What happens if kererū populations decline?
- How does kauri dieback disease affect the entire ecosystem?
- Which species are keystone species and why?

**Extension**: Research how Māori traditionally understood these relationships through kōrero and observations.

---

### 🌡️ **Activity 2: Human Impact Analysis**

**Time**: 35 minutes | **Materials**: Data sheets, calculators, maps

**Scenarios to Analyze**:

**1. Wetland Conversion (1840-2024)**

- **1840**: 670,000 hectares of wetlands in NZ
- **2024**: 90,000 hectares remaining (13% of original)
- **Current loss rate**: 0.5% per year

**2. Predator Impact Data**

- **Rats**: Kill 25 million native birds annually
- **Stoats**: Primary threat to kiwi chicks (95% mortality)
- **Cats**: Impact on 37 native bird species

**Tasks**:

1. Calculate percentage wetland loss over time
2. Graph predator impact trends
3. Predict ecosystem changes if current trends continue
4. Design conservation strategies based on data

**🚩 Cultural Connection**: Research traditional Māori practices for ecosystem management and protection.

---

### 🔬 **Activity 3: Ecosystem Health Assessment**

**Time**: 25 minutes | **Materials**: pH strips, thermometers, field guides

**Field Study Protocol** (or simulation):

1. **Water Quality Testing**:
   - pH levels (healthy: 6.5-8.5)
   - Temperature monitoring
   - Dissolved oxygen levels

2. **Biodiversity Survey**:
   - Count native vs introduced species
   - Identify indicator species (healthy ecosystem markers)
   - Record signs of stress or disease

3. **Human Impact Evidence**:
   - Pollution indicators
   - Habitat fragmentation
   - Invasive species presence

**Data Analysis**: Compare your findings to baseline healthy ecosystem data. What conclusions can you draw?

## Cultural Connections

### 🌿 **Mātauranga Māori and Ecology**

**🚩 CULTURAL CONTENT FLAG**: The following content includes traditional Māori knowledge that should be reviewed for cultural accuracy and appropriateness.

**Traditional Ecological Knowledge**:

- **Mauri**: The life force present in all natural elements
- **Whakapapa**: Genealogical connections between all living things
- **Kaitiakitanga**: Guardianship and sustainable management of natural resources
- **Tapu and noa**: Sacred and ordinary states affecting resource use

### 🏛️ **Traditional Management Practices**

**Rāhui**: Traditional conservation method

- Temporary restrictions on harvesting in specific areas
- Allows species populations to recover
- Community-enforced sustainability measure

**Rongoā Māori**: Traditional medicine from native plants

- Deep knowledge of plant properties and ecosystem relationships
- Sustainable harvesting practices
- Connection between human and environmental health

**Cultural Safety Note**: These concepts represent living knowledge systems. Students should approach with respect and understanding that this is Māori intellectual property requiring appropriate protocols.

## Extension Activities

### 🌍 **Comparative Ecosystems**

1. **International Comparisons**: Compare NZ ecosystems with similar environments globally
2. **Evolution and Isolation**: Investigate how NZ's geographic isolation shaped unique species
3. **Climate Change Impacts**: Model how warming temperatures affect alpine and coastal ecosystems

### 📊 **Data Collection Projects**

1. **Local Ecosystem Survey**: Study your school grounds or local park ecosystem
2. **Citizen Science**: Contribute to national biodiversity databases (iNaturalist NZ)
3. **Historical Research**: Use old photos/maps to track ecosystem changes in your area

### 🎯 **Action Projects**

1. **School Native Garden**: Design and plant a native ecosystem garden
2. **Predator Control**: Research and propose predator management for local areas
3. **Community Education**: Create presentations for younger students about ecosystem protection

## Assessment Opportunities

### **Formative Assessment**

- **Exit Ticket**: "Name one way native species depend on each other"
- **Ecosystem Role Play**: Students act as different species explaining their ecosystem role
- **Quick Draw**: Sketch a simple food chain from today's learning

### **Summative Assessment**

- **Ecosystem Report**: Choose a NZ ecosystem and analyze its health and threats
- **Food Web Model**: Create detailed food web showing energy flows and relationships
- **Conservation Proposal**: Design evidence-based plan to protect a local ecosystem

### **Reflection Questions**

- How do traditional Māori practices compare to modern conservation methods?
- What responsibility do humans have to protect native ecosystems?
- How might climate change affect the ecosystems we studied today?
