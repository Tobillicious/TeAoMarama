# Nutrition and Wellbeing: Kai for Life - Year 8

*Understanding healthy eating through New Zealand food cultures*

## Learning Intention

**WALT**: We are learning to make informed decisions about nutrition using knowledge of diverse food cultures in Aotearoa
**Success Criteria**:

- I can identify nutrients needed for adolescent health and development
- I can analyze the nutritional value of traditional and contemporary New Zealand foods
- I can plan balanced meals that reflect cultural diversity and personal preferences
- I can explain how food choices impact both personal and environmental health

## Nutrition Analysis Activities

### 🍎 **Activity 1: Cultural Food Analysis**

**Time**: 30 minutes | **Materials**: Food packaging, nutrition apps, cultural food guides

**Traditional vs Modern Comparison** (15 minutes):

1. **Choose a cultural food tradition** (Māori, Pacific, Asian, European, etc.):
   - List 5 traditional foods from this culture
   - Research their nutritional content
   - Compare with modern processed alternatives
   - Calculate nutritional differences

2. **Example Analysis**:
   - Traditional: Fresh fish vs Fish and chips
   - Nutrients: Protein, omega-3, vitamins vs calories, saturated fat, sodium
   - Cultural context: How preparation methods affect nutrition

**Modern Adaptation Investigation** (15 minutes):
3. **Interview family/community members**:

- How have family food traditions changed over generations?
- What traditional foods are still regularly eaten?
- What challenges exist in maintaining traditional food practices?
- How do families balance tradition with convenience?

**Reflection Questions**:

- What nutritional benefits might be lost when traditional foods are replaced?
- How can traditional food wisdom inform modern healthy eating?

---

### 📊 **Activity 2: Personal Nutrition Assessment**

**Time**: 35 minutes | **Materials**: Food diary template, nutrition analysis tools

**3-Day Food Diary Analysis** (20 minutes):

1. **Record everything consumed** for 3 days (including weekends):
   - Meals, snacks, drinks
   - Portion sizes (use hand measurements if needed)
   - Eating contexts (home, school, social, etc.)
   - Cultural foods vs processed foods ratio

2. **Nutritional Analysis**:
   - Calculate daily averages for key nutrients
   - Compare with recommended intakes for adolescents
   - Identify nutritional strengths and gaps
   - Analyze eating patterns and triggers

**Goal Setting and Planning** (15 minutes):
3. **Improvement Plan Development**:

- Choose 2-3 realistic nutritional improvements
- Plan specific actions (e.g., "add one extra vegetable serving daily")
- Include cultural foods that appeal to you
- Consider practical constraints (budget, time, family preferences)

**Cultural Integration**: Include traditional foods from your cultural background in improvement plans.

---

### 🌱 **Activity 3: Sustainable Food Choices**

**Time**: 25 minutes | **Materials**: Local food guides, environmental impact data

**Local Food Investigation** (15 minutes):

1. **New Zealand Seasonal Food Calendar**:
   - Research what foods are in season now
   - Compare nutritional content of seasonal vs out-of-season produce
   - Calculate food miles for local vs imported foods
   - Investigate local food production in your region

2. **Environmental Impact Analysis**:
   - Compare carbon footprint: beef vs chicken vs plant proteins
   - Water usage: different food production methods
   - Packaging: fresh vs processed food environmental costs
   - Transport: local vs international food sources

**Practical Application** (10 minutes):
3. **Sustainable Meal Planning**:

- Design a week's meals using primarily local, seasonal foods
- Include foods from multiple cultural traditions available in NZ
- Balance nutrition, cultural preferences, and environmental impact
- Consider budget constraints and cooking skills

**Community Connections**: Research local farmers' markets, community gardens, or cultural food cooperatives.

## Health and Wellbeing Integration

### 💪 **Adolescent Nutrition Needs**

**Growth and Development Requirements**:

- **Protein**: 0.85g per kg body weight daily for muscle development
- **Calcium**: 1300mg daily for bone strength during peak bone building years
- **Iron**: 11mg (males), 15mg (females) for blood health and energy
- **Vitamin D**: Adequate sunlight exposure plus dietary sources for bone health
- **Omega-3 fatty acids**: Brain development and mental health support

**Common Adolescent Nutrition Challenges**:

- **Irregular eating patterns**: Skipping meals, especially breakfast
- **Social eating influences**: Peer pressure affecting food choices
- **Body image concerns**: Unhealthy dieting or eating behaviors
- **Energy demands**: Balancing nutrition with high activity levels
- **Independence**: Learning to make food choices without parent supervision

### 🧠 **Mental Health and Nutrition Connections**

**Foods Supporting Mental Wellbeing**:

- **Complex carbohydrates**: Steady blood sugar for mood stability
- **Omega-3 rich foods**: Fish, nuts, seeds for brain health
- **Probiotic foods**: Fermented foods for gut-brain health connection
- **Antioxidant-rich foods**: Berries, leafy greens for stress resilience

**Cultural Approaches to Food and Wellbeing**:

- **Māori perspective**: Kai as medicine, connection to land and sea
- **Pacific traditions**: Community eating for social wellbeing
- **Asian concepts**: Balance and harmony in food combinations
- **Mediterranean approaches**: Social meals and fresh, varied ingredients

## Teacher Notes

### **Cultural Sensitivity Requirements**

- **Community consultation**: Involve cultural advisors for traditional food knowledge
- **Respectful representation**: Ensure accurate portrayal of cultural food practices
- **Family sensitivity**: Be aware that food insecurity affects some students
- **Body image awareness**: Maintain focus on health rather than appearance

### **Practical Considerations**

- **Food allergies**: Survey students for dietary restrictions before activities
- **Budget awareness**: Ensure activities are accessible regardless of family income
- **Cultural dietary requirements**: Respect religious and cultural eating restrictions
- **Kitchen access**: Adapt activities based on available cooking facilities

### **Differentiation Strategies**

- **Visual learners**: Use food models, charts, and infographics
- **Kinesthetic learners**: Emphasize hands-on cooking and food preparation
- **Advanced students**: Include biochemistry of nutrition and metabolism
- **Support students**: Provide simplified nutrition concepts and practical focus
- **ELL students**: Use visual aids and hands-on activities to support understanding
