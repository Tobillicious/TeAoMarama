## 🏛️ **Content Structure**

### **1. Introduction to Architectural Geometry (10 minutes)**

**What is Architectural Geometry?**

- The study of geometric shapes and patterns in buildings and structures
- How mathematical principles guide design and construction
- The relationship between form, function, and aesthetics

**NZ Architectural Context**:

- Traditional and modern building styles
- Geometric patterns in Māori and European architecture
- Environmental considerations in design
- Cultural influences on geometric choices

### **2. Basic Geometric Shapes in Architecture (15 minutes)**

**Two-Dimensional Shapes**:

- **Rectangles**: Building footprints, windows, doors
- **Squares**: Courtyards, floor tiles, decorative elements
- **Triangles**: Roof structures, gables, support beams
- **Circles**: Round buildings, windows, decorative features
- **Polygons**: Complex building shapes, floor plans

**Three-Dimensional Shapes**:

- **Cubes**: Square buildings, storage units
- **Rectangular Prisms**: Most buildings, rooms
- **Cylinders**: Round towers, silos, water tanks
- **Pyramids**: Roof structures, decorative elements
- **Spheres**: Domes, decorative features

### **3. Geometric Measurements and Calculations (20 minutes)**

**Area Calculations**:

- **Floor Area**: Length × Width for rectangular spaces
- **Wall Area**: Height × Perimeter for room surfaces
- **Roof Area**: Sloped surface calculations using triangles
- **Window and Door Areas**: Subtracting from wall areas

**Perimeter and Volume**:

- **Building Perimeter**: Distance around the building
- **Room Volume**: Length × Width × Height
- **Storage Capacity**: Volume calculations for practical use
- **Material Requirements**: Area and volume for construction

**Practical Applications**:

- Calculating paint needed for walls
- Determining flooring material quantities
- Estimating heating/cooling requirements
- Planning furniture placement

### **4. Design and Problem Solving (15 minutes)**

**Architectural Design Challenges**:

- **Space Planning**: Optimizing room layouts
- **Material Efficiency**: Minimizing waste in construction
- **Energy Efficiency**: Geometric factors affecting heating/cooling
- **Aesthetic Balance**: Using geometric principles for beauty

**NZ Design Examples**:

- **Modern Homes**: Open-plan living with geometric flow
- **Commercial Buildings**: Efficient use of space and materials
- **Public Spaces**: Geometric patterns in parks and plazas
- **Cultural Buildings**: Traditional and contemporary geometric elements

## 🎮 **Learning Activities**

### **Activity 1: Building Geometry Hunt**

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Materials**: Building photos, geometric shape cards, measurement tools

**Instructions**:

1. Students examine photos of NZ buildings
2. Identify geometric shapes in the architecture
3. Classify shapes by type and properties
4. Measure and calculate areas of visible elements
5. Discuss how geometry affects building function

**Focus Areas**:

- **Shape Identification**: Rectangles, triangles, circles, polygons
- **Property Analysis**: Sides, angles, symmetry, patterns
- **Measurement Practice**: Using rulers, protractors, calculators
- **Design Discussion**: How geometry serves function

### **Activity 2: Room Design Project**

**Duration**: 25 minutes
**Materials**: Graph paper, rulers, calculators, design briefs

**Instructions**:

1. Students receive a room design brief
2. Create a floor plan using geometric shapes
3. Calculate area, perimeter, and volume
4. Plan furniture placement using geometric principles
5. Present design with mathematical justification

**Design Challenges**:

- **Bedroom Design**: Efficient use of space
- **Classroom Layout**: Optimal learning environment
- **Kitchen Planning**: Work triangle and storage
- **Office Space**: Productivity and comfort

### **Activity 3: Architectural Scale Model**

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Materials**: Building blocks, geometric shapes, measurement tools

**Instructions**:

1. Build a simple architectural model
2. Use geometric shapes to create structure
3. Measure and calculate model dimensions
4. Scale up to real-world measurements
5. Discuss geometric principles in construction

**Model Types**:

- **Simple House**: Basic geometric forms
- **Commercial Building**: Complex geometric relationships
- **Bridge Structure**: Geometric strength principles
- **Public Space**: Geometric flow and function
