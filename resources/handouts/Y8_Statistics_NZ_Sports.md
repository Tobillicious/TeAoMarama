## Data Analysis Activities

### 📊 **Activity 1: Central Tendency Champions**

**Time**: 25 minutes | **Materials**: Calculator, graph paper

**All Blacks Win Percentage Analysis**:

1. **Mean**: Calculate the average win percentage from 2019-2024
2. **Median**: Find the middle value when win percentages are arranged in order
3. **Mode**: Identify if any win percentage appears more than once
4. **Range**: What's the difference between highest and lowest win percentages?

**Questions for Discussion**:

- Which measure best represents All Blacks performance? Why?
- What factors might explain the variation in performance?
- How might COVID-19 have affected 2020 results?

---

### 📈 **Activity 2: Trend Analysis and Predictions**

**Time**: 30 minutes | **Materials**: Graph paper, ruler, colored pencils

**Create Line Graphs**:

1. Plot All Blacks win percentages over time (2019-2024)
2. Plot Football Ferns world ranking positions over time
3. Plot Black Ferns world ranking positions over time

**Analysis Tasks**:

- Identify trends: Which teams are improving/declining?
- Make predictions: What might 2025 rankings look like?
- Compare: How do men's vs women's rugby rankings differ?

**Extension**: Research what major tournaments happened in each year and see if they correlate with ranking changes.

---

### 🥇 **Activity 3: Olympic Success Analysis**

**Time**: 35 minutes | **Materials**: Calculator, chart paper

**Medal Analysis**:

1. Calculate total medals for each Olympics
2. Find the percentage of medals won in each sport category
3. Create a pie chart showing medal distribution by sport
4. Compare medal efficiency: medals per athlete sent

**Cultural Connections**:

- Research which athletes are Māori, Pacific, or other cultural backgrounds
- How does NZ's small population compare to medal success vs other countries?
- What sports have traditional connections to Māori culture?

## Statistical Thinking Questions

### 🤔 **Critical Analysis**

1. **Sample Size**: Why might 2020 data be less reliable than other years?
2. **Bias**: Do these statistics tell the whole story of NZ sports success?
3. **Correlation vs Causation**: If a team's ranking improves, does that mean they're definitely playing better?

### 🌍 **Real-World Applications**

1. **Coaching Decisions**: How might coaches use win/loss data to make decisions?
2. **Funding**: Should government funding be based on world rankings or medal potential?
3. **Community Sport**: How do elite sports statistics relate to community participation?

## Cultural Context: Sport in Aotearoa

### 🏛️ **Māori and Pacific Contributions**

New Zealand's sporting success has deep cultural roots:

- **Haka**: The All Blacks' pre-match haka connects to Māori warrior traditions
- **Pacific Power**: Many of our rugby stars have Pacific Island heritage
- **Community Values**: Concepts like whānau (family) and manaakitanga (hospitality) influence team culture

### 🌊 **Traditional Sports**

Before European contact, Māori had many traditional sports:

- **Ki-o-rahi**: Traditional ball game with complex scoring
- **Poi**: Developing hand-eye coordination and rhythm
- **Waka racing**: Canoe racing connecting to navigation traditions

**Research Extension**: Investigate how traditional Māori sports connect to modern athletic training.
