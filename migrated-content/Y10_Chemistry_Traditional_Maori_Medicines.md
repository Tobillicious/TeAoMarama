## 🌿 Cultural Foundation / Tūāpapa Rongoā

### **Core Traditional Medicine Concepts**:

#### **Rongoā Māori Philosophy**
- **Holistic healing**: Mind, body, spirit, and whānau
- **Whakapapa connections**: Relationship with atua, ancestors, and whenua
- **Tohunga rongoa**: Highly trained traditional healers
- **Mauri**: Life force in plants and healing processes
- **Tikanga**: Cultural protocols governing healing practices

#### **Traditional Preparation Methods**
- **Kohu**: Steam inhalation from heated plants
- **Inu**: Internal consumption of plant preparations
- **Takai**: External application as poultices
- **Horoi**: Washing with medicinal plant waters
- **Whakamā**: Specific preparation rituals and processes

#### **Cultural Safety in Learning**
- **Educational appreciation only**: Students learn about, not practice, traditional healing
- **Respect for practitioners**: Acknowledge the skill and training of tohunga rongoa
- **Sacred knowledge boundaries**: Some knowledge is not appropriate for general education
- **Community benefit**: Learning serves to appreciate and support traditional knowledge
- **Cultural protocols**: Following proper acknowledgment and attribution practices

## 🧪 Unit Content Structure

### **Week 1: Te Taiao - Plant Chemistry Foundations**

#### **Lesson 1: Introduction to Natural Product Chemistry**
- **Chemical families** in medicinal plants (alkaloids, terpenes, phenolics)
- **Molecular structures** and biological activity
- **Traditional observation** vs modern chemical analysis
- **Cultural context** of plant selection and use

**Traditional Plants Studied (with cultural protocols)**:
- **Kawa kawa** (Macropiper excelsum) - Pain relief compounds
- **Mānuka** (Leptospermum scoparium) - Antimicrobial properties
- **Harakeke** (Phormium tenax) - Wound healing applications
- **Karamu** (Coprosma robusta) - Digestive aid chemistry

**Chemistry Focus**:
- Molecular formulas and structures
- Functional groups and properties
- Structure-activity relationships
- Natural vs synthetic compounds

**Cultural Learning**:
- Traditional knowledge systems and observation methods
- Relationship between people and plants in Māori worldview
- Seasonal collection and sustainable harvesting principles
- Whakapapa of plants and their healing properties

#### **Lesson 2: Extraction and Solubility Principles**
- **Traditional methods**: Hot water, cold pressing, fermentation
- **Modern techniques**: Distillation, chromatography, crystallization
- **Polarity and solubility**: Like dissolves like principle
- **Concentration and bioavailability**

**Practical Activity**: 
Safe extraction of compounds from mānuka leaves using water and ethanol, comparing traditional and modern methods

**Cultural Connection**:
How traditional healers developed sophisticated understanding of extraction without modern chemistry knowledge

---

### **Week 2: Ngā Rau Rongoā - Active Compounds and Analysis**

#### **Lesson 3: Alkaloid Chemistry in Medicinal Plants**
- **Alkaloid structures** and basic chemistry
- **Pharmacological effects** and mechanism of action
- **Biosynthesis** in plants as natural defense
- **Isolation and purification** techniques

**Case Study**: 
**Kawakawa alkaloids** - Traditional pain relief and modern pharmaceutical development

**Laboratory Investigation**:
- Test pH of plant extracts (alkaloids are basic)
- Observe color changes with indicator solutions
- Compare alkaloid content in different plant parts

**Traditional Knowledge Integration**:
- How traditional healers identified potent plants
- Seasonal variation in plant chemistry
- Preparation methods that optimize alkaloid extraction

#### **Lesson 4: Antimicrobial Compounds and Testing**
- **Phenolic compounds** and their antimicrobial properties
- **Essential oils** and volatile organic compounds
- **Mechanism of antimicrobial action**
- **Minimum inhibitory concentration** concepts

**Featured Traditional Medicine**:
**Mānuka honey and essential oil** - Traditional wound treatment with proven antimicrobial activity

**Practical Investigation**:
- Test antimicrobial activity of plant extracts using safe bacterial cultures
- Compare effectiveness of different extraction methods
- Analyze concentration-effect relationships

**Cultural Context**:
- Traditional wound care and infection prevention
- Oral traditions preserving antimicrobial knowledge
- Integration with modern medical practice

---

### **Week 3: Ngā Taonga Atua - Preparation Chemistry**

#### **Lesson 5: Solution Chemistry and Traditional Preparations**
- **Concentration calculations** for safe and effective doses
- **Solution preparation** and dilution principles
- **pH effects** on compound stability and activity
- **Storage and preservation** chemistry

**Traditional Preparation Analysis**:
- **Kohu (steam)**: Volatile compound extraction
- **Inu (drink)**: Water-soluble compound extraction
- **Takai (poultice)**: Direct compound application

**Laboratory Skills**:
- Prepare solutions of known concentration
- Calculate dilutions for different applications
- Test pH and adjust if necessary
- Analyze extraction efficiency

**Cultural Learning**:
- Traditional measurement systems and dosing
- Preparation rituals and their practical benefits
- Seasonal timing for optimal compound levels
- Knowledge transmission through apprenticeship

#### **Lesson 6: Chemical Reactions in Traditional Processing**
- **Fermentation chemistry** and beneficial chemical changes
- **Oxidation and preservation** methods
- **Heat effects** on compound stability
- **pH changes** during processing

**Case Study Investigation**:
**Traditional kūmara preparation** - How fermentation changes nutrient chemistry and digestibility

**Experimental Design**:
Students design experiments to test how different processing methods affect plant compound activity

**Traditional Knowledge Connections**:
- Why certain preparation methods were developed
- Seasonal processing and storage techniques
- Chemical understanding behind traditional practices

## 🔬 Practical Investigations / Rangahau Matū

### **Investigation 1: Comparative Extraction Efficiency**
Students compare traditional and modern extraction methods:
- **Hypothesis**: Traditional methods may be as effective as modern techniques
- **Method**: Extract compounds from kawakawa using traditional and modern methods
- **Analysis**: Compare yield, purity, and bioactivity
- **Cultural Integration**: Understanding why traditional methods were developed

### **Investigation 2: Antimicrobial Activity Testing**
Safe assessment of antimicrobial compounds:
- **Plant selection**: Mānuka, kawakawa, karamu extracts
- **Method**: Disc diffusion assays with safe bacterial cultures
- **Variables**: Concentration, extraction method, storage time
- **Applications**: Understanding traditional infection control

### **Investigation 3: pH and Stability Analysis**
How preparation methods affect compound stability:
- **Focus**: Active compounds in different pH conditions
- **Methods**: Spectroscopic analysis of compound degradation
- **Traditional connection**: Why certain preparation methods preserve activity
- **Real-world application**: Modern product formulation
