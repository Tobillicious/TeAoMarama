# Year 6 Technology: Sustainable Community Design Challenge
**New Zealand Curriculum Level 3-4**

## Learning Objectives
Students will be able to:
- Apply design thinking process to solve real community problems
- Use digital tools to create and communicate design solutions
- Understand principles of sustainability and environmental responsibility
- Collaborate effectively in design teams
- Present and justify design decisions to authentic audiences

---

## Unit Overview (8 lessons + 2 showcase sessions)

This unit challenges students to design sustainable solutions for their local community, integrating design thinking, digital technologies, and environmental awareness while connecting to authentic local contexts.

---

## Lesson 1: Understanding Our Community's Challenges

### Hook Activity (15 minutes)
**"Community Walk-Through"**

Virtual or actual community exploration:
- Use Google Earth/Street View to examine local area
- Identify different community spaces: parks, shops, schools, transport
- Take photos or screenshots of areas that could be improved

**Guiding Questions:**
- What do you notice about how people move around our community?
- Where do you see environmental challenges?
- What spaces look unused or could be better designed?

### Community Challenge Research (25 minutes)
**"Real Problems, Real Solutions"**

Students research actual community challenges relevant to New Zealand:

**Challenge Categories:**
1. **Sustainable Transport**
   - How can we reduce car dependency?
   - Designing safe cycling/walking routes
   - Electric vehicle charging stations

2. **Waste Reduction**
   - Community composting systems
   - Plastic-free initiatives
   - Upcycling and repair hubs

3. **Water Conservation**
   - Rainwater collection systems
   - Greywater gardens
   - Stream restoration projects

4. **Energy Efficiency**
   - Solar power for community buildings
   - Wind power solutions
   - Energy-saving community initiatives

5. **Food Security**
   - Community gardens and food forests
   - Kai preservation and sharing systems
   - School-to-table programs

6. **Cultural Connection**
   - Māori cultural learning spaces
   - Intergenerational knowledge sharing
   - Community storytelling areas

### Team Formation and Challenge Selection (10 minutes)
**Design Teams**

Form teams of 3-4 students based on:
- Interest in specific challenge areas
- Mix of different strengths (creative, technical, research, communication)
- Collaborative working preferences

**Team Roles:**
- **Project Manager:** Keeps team on track, manages timeline
- **Research Specialist:** Gathers information and user feedback
- **Design Engineer:** Focuses on technical solutions and prototyping
- **Communications Lead:** Handles presentations and community liaison

## Lesson 2: Understanding Users and Stakeholders

### Empathy Mapping Activity (20 minutes)
**"Who Are We Designing For?"**

Teams create empathy maps for different community stakeholders:

**Stakeholder Examples:**
- Primary school children walking to school
- Elderly residents accessing community services
- Local business owners managing waste
- Māori whānau wanting cultural connection
- Families growing their own food
- Cyclists needing safe routes

**Empathy Map Questions:**
- What do they THINK? (concerns, beliefs, values)
- What do they FEEL? (emotions, feelings)
- What do they SEE? (environment, influences)
- What do they SAY/DO? (actions, behaviors)
- PAINS: What frustrates them?
- GAINS: What would make their life better?

### Community Interview Planning (15 minutes)
**"Asking the Right Questions"**

Teams develop interview questions for community members:

**Interview Question Categories:**
1. **Current Experience:** "Tell us about how you currently..."
2. **Challenges:** "What makes it difficult to..."
3. **Ideal Solutions:** "If you could change one thing..."
4. **Cultural Considerations:** "What's important to you about..."
5. **Environmental Concerns:** "How do you think about sustainability..."

**Interview Methods:**
- Video calls with family members
- Email surveys to school community
- Interviews with school staff
- Community group representatives

### Homework Assignment (5 minutes)
**Real Community Research**

Each team conducts 2-3 interviews with community stakeholders before next lesson.

## Lesson 3: Design Thinking - Define and Ideate

### Interview Insights Sharing (15 minutes)
**"What Did We Learn?"**

Teams share key insights from community interviews:
- Create "How Might We..." statements
- Identify common themes and surprising discoveries
- Prioritize most important user needs

**Example "How Might We" Statements:**
- How might we make it safer for children to walk to school?
- How might we help whānau connect with traditional kai practices?
- How might we reduce plastic waste in our school lunches?

### Brainstorming Session (20 minutes)
**"Crazy Ideas Welcome!"**

Use structured brainstorming techniques:

**Round 1: Individual Brainstorming (5 minutes)**
- Silent idea generation using sticky notes
- Quantity over quality - aim for 20+ ideas
- No criticism or evaluation

**Round 2: Building on Ideas (10 minutes)**
- Share ideas within team
- Build on others' suggestions
- Combine different concepts

**Round 3: Wild Ideas (5 minutes)**
- "What if we had unlimited money/resources?"
- "What would a solution from the future look like?"
- Push boundaries and think creatively

### Idea Evaluation and Selection (15 minutes)
**"Which Ideas Have the Most Potential?"**

Evaluation criteria:
- **Impact:** How much would this help users?
- **Feasibility:** Can we actually create this?
- **Sustainability:** Is this environmentally responsible?
- **Cultural Appropriateness:** Does this respect our community values?
- **Innovation:** How creative and original is this?

Teams select top 2-3 ideas for further development.

## Lesson 4: Digital Design and Prototyping

### Introduction to Design Tools (10 minutes)
**"Digital Tools for Real Design"**

Introduce age-appropriate design software:
- **Tinkercad:** 3D modeling and design
- **Scratch/Blockly:** Programming interactive elements
- **Canva/Book Creator:** Visual design and presentation
- **SketchUp Free:** Architectural and space design
- **MIT App Inventor:** Simple app creation

### Digital Prototype Creation (30 minutes)
**"Bringing Ideas to Life"**

Teams create digital prototypes based on their chosen solution:

**3D Models (Tinkercad):**
- Community structures (bike shelters, compost bins, solar installations)
- Product designs (reusable containers, rain collection systems)
- Playground or garden layouts

**Interactive Presentations (Scratch/Canva):**
- Animated explanations of how solutions work
- Interactive decision trees for community choices
- Digital posters with embedded videos

**Virtual Spaces (SketchUp):**
- Redesigned community areas
- Sustainable building designs
- Cultural learning environments

**Simple Apps (MIT App Inventor):**
- Community resource sharing platforms
- Environmental impact calculators
- Cultural knowledge apps

### Peer Feedback Session (10 minutes)
**"Critical Friends Protocol"**

Teams rotate to provide feedback on digital prototypes:
- What works well in this design?
- What questions do you have about how it works?
- What suggestions do you have for improvement?
- How could this be even more sustainable/culturally appropriate?

## Lesson 5: Testing and Iteration

### User Testing with Community Members (25 minutes)
**"Does This Actually Work for Real People?"**

Teams present their digital prototypes to real community stakeholders via:
- Video calls with interviewees from Lesson 2
- Presentations to other classes or school staff
- Family member feedback sessions
- Community group presentations (if possible)

**Testing Protocol:**
1. **Explain the Problem:** "We're trying to solve..."
2. **Show the Solution:** Demonstrate digital prototype
3. **Gather Feedback:** "What do you think? How could this be better?"
4. **Ask Specific Questions:** About usability, effectiveness, cultural appropriateness

### Iteration and Improvement (20 minutes)
**"Making It Better Based on Real Feedback"**

Teams revise their digital prototypes based on user feedback:
- Identify top 3 pieces of feedback to address
- Make concrete improvements to digital designs
- Add new features or modify existing ones
- Prepare for final presentation

### Reflection on Design Process (5 minutes)
**"What Are We Learning About Design?"**

Individual reflection questions:
- How has your solution changed since you first thought of it?
- What was the most valuable feedback you received?
- What's been challenging about the design process?
- How do you feel about presenting to real community members?

## Lesson 6: Sustainability and Environmental Impact Assessment

### Life Cycle Analysis Activity (20 minutes)
**"What's the Real Environmental Impact?"**

Teams analyze their solutions using simplified life cycle thinking:

**Materials:**
- What materials would be needed?
- Where would these come from?
- Are they renewable or recyclable?

**Energy:**
- How much energy would the solution use?
- Could it use renewable energy sources?
- Would it actually save energy overall?

**Waste:**
- What waste would be created during construction/use?
- How could waste be minimized?
- What happens at the end of its useful life?

**Transport:**
- How would materials get to the site?
- Would the solution reduce transport needs?
- Local vs. imported components?

### Sustainability Scorecard (15 minutes)
**"Rating Our Environmental Responsibility"**

Teams create sustainability scorecards for their solutions:

| Criteria | Poor (1) | Good (3) | Excellent (5) | Our Score |
|----------|----------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Materials** | Uses lots of non-renewable materials | Some sustainable materials | Mostly recycled/renewable materials | |
| **Energy** | High energy use | Moderate energy use | Energy-positive or very low use | |
| **Waste** | Creates significant waste | Some waste reduction | Zero waste or beneficial waste | |
| **Longevity** | Short-term solution | Medium-term durability | Long-lasting, adaptable solution | |
| **Local Impact** | Benefits few people | Benefits some community members | Benefits whole community | |

### Improvement Planning (15 minutes)
**"How Can We Make It Even More Sustainable?"**

Based on scorecard results, teams plan improvements:
- What's our lowest score and how can we improve it?
- Are there alternative materials or approaches?
- How can we maximize positive environmental impact?
- What would make this solution even better for our community?

## Lesson 7: Cultural Responsiveness and Community Values

### Māori Perspectives on Design (15 minutes)
**"Te Taiao - Environment and Relationships"**

Explore Māori concepts relevant to sustainable design:

**Key Concepts:**
- **Whakatōhea:** Collective responsibility for environment
- **Kaitiakitanga:** Guardianship and sustainable resource management
- **Whakapapa:** Connections between all living things
- **Mauri:** Life force in natural systems
- **Tapu:** Respect for sacred aspects of environment

**Discussion Questions:**
- How does your solution honor the mauri (life force) of natural systems?
- What responsibilities do we have as kaitiaki (guardians) of our environment?
- How does your design consider the whakapapa (connections) in the community?

### Cultural Impact Assessment (20 minutes)
**"Respecting Our Bicultural Foundation"**

Teams evaluate their solutions for cultural appropriateness:

**Assessment Questions:**
1. **Inclusion:** Would this solution be accessible and welcoming to all cultural groups?
2. **Respect:** Does this acknowledge and respect Māori tino rangatiratanga?
3. **Partnership:** Have we involved Māori perspectives in our design process?
4. **Representation:** Do our materials and designs reflect cultural diversity respectfully?
5. **Benefit:** Would this genuinely benefit Māori and other cultural communities?

### Community Values Integration (15 minutes)
**"What Matters Most to Our Community?"**

Teams identify and integrate key community values:
- **Environmental stewardship**
- **Cultural respect and celebration**
- **Intergenerational connection**
- **Social equity and inclusion**
- **Economic sustainability**
- **Health and wellbeing**

Revise solutions to better reflect these values.

## Lesson 8: Final Presentation Preparation

### Presentation Planning (20 minutes)
**"Telling Our Design Story"**

Teams plan 5-minute presentations including:

**Structure:**
1. **Problem Introduction (1 minute):** What community challenge did you tackle?
2. **Design Process (1.5 minutes):** How did you research and develop your solution?
3. **Solution Demonstration (2 minutes):** Show your digital prototype in action
4. **Impact and Sustainability (1 minute):** How will this help the community and environment?
5. **Next Steps (0.5 minutes):** How could this actually be implemented?

**Presentation Requirements:**
- Everyone in the team speaks
- Digital prototype is clearly demonstrated
- Evidence of community consultation included
- Sustainability considerations explained
- Cultural responsiveness addressed

### Technical Rehearsal (20 minutes)
**"Making Sure Technology Works"**

Teams practice:
- Loading and running digital prototypes smoothly
- Screen sharing and presentation technology
- Backup plans if technology fails
- Timing and transitions between speakers

### Peer Feedback and Final Improvements (10 minutes)
**"Last Chance to Make It Even Better"**

Teams provide final feedback to each other:
- Clarity of problem and solution explanation
- Effectiveness of digital prototype demonstration
- Strength of community and sustainability evidence
- Suggestions for presentation improvement

## Lesson 9: Community Showcase Presentation

### Presentation Day Setup (10 minutes)
**"Professional Presentation Environment"**

Create professional presentation environment:
- Arrange seating for community guests
- Test all technology and projection
- Brief students on presentation etiquette
- Review evaluation criteria with audience

### Team Presentations (40 minutes)
**"Sharing Our Solutions with the Community"**

Each team presents to authentic audience including:
- Community members who were interviewed
- School leadership and staff
- Parents/whānau members
- Local council representatives (if possible)
- Other classes as supportive audience

**Audience Evaluation Focus:**
- Clarity of problem identification
- Creativity and feasibility of solution
- Quality of community consultation
- Sustainability and environmental consideration
- Cultural responsiveness and respect
- Professional presentation skills

## Lesson 10: Reflection and Next Steps

### Individual Reflection (15 minutes)
**"What Did I Learn About Design and Technology?"**

Written reflection addressing:
- How has your understanding of design thinking changed?
- What digital skills did you develop or improve?
- What surprised you about working with real community members?
- How do you now think about sustainability and environmental responsibility?
- What would you do differently if you started this project again?

### Team Achievement Celebration (15 minutes)
**"Celebrating Our Collaborative Success"**

Teams identify and celebrate:
- Biggest challenge overcome together
- Most creative moment in the design process
- Best example of effective collaboration
- Proudest aspect of final solution
- Most valuable feedback received from community

### Action Planning (15 minutes)
**"Could This Actually Happen?"**

Teams consider realistic next steps:
- What would it take to actually implement this solution?
- Who in the community would need to be involved?
- What resources would be required?
- How could students continue to be involved?
- What smaller first steps could be taken?

### Unit Celebration (5 minutes)
**"Design Thinkers and Community Problem Solvers"**

Celebrate development of:
- Digital technology skills
- Design thinking capabilities
- Community engagement experience
- Environmental consciousness
- Cultural responsiveness
- Collaborative problem-solving abilities

## Differentiation Strategies

### For Advanced Students
- **Mentor role** supporting teams with technical challenges
- **Extended research** into professional design processes
- **Complex digital tools** like 3D printing design or advanced programming
- **Leadership opportunities** in community liaison and presentation coordination

### For Students Needing Support
- **Simplified digital tools** with guided templates
- **Structured role** within team matching their strengths
- **Additional scaffolding** for design process documentation
- **Choice in presentation** format (visual, demonstration, collaborative speaking)

### For English Language Learners
- **Visual design process** templates and graphic organizers
- **Multilingual resources** for technical vocabulary
- **Peer translation support** during community interviews
- **Cultural knowledge** sharing from diverse backgrounds
