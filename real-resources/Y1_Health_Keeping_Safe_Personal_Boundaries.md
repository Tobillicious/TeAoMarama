# Year 1 Health: Keeping Safe - Understanding Personal Boundaries

**New Zealand Curriculum Level 1**

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

- Understand what personal safety means
- Identify trusted adults who can help them
- Recognize the difference between appropriate and inappropriate touch
- Know how to say "no" and get help when needed
- Understand basic body safety rules

---

## Important Note for Teachers

This is a sensitive topic requiring careful, age-appropriate delivery. Please:

- **Consult your school's child protection policy** before beginning
- **Inform parents/caregivers** about this unit in advance
- **Follow your school's procedures** for any disclosures
- **Keep lessons positive and empowering**, not frightening
- **Be available for individual conversations** if needed

## Lesson 1: My Body Belongs to Me (30 minutes)

### Circle Time Introduction (10 minutes)

**"All About Me" Discussion**

Sit in circle and discuss:

- "What makes you special and unique?"
- "Who are the people who love and care for you?"
- "What does it mean to feel safe?"

**Key Message:** "Your body belongs to you. You are special and important."

### Story Time (10 minutes)

**"My Body is Special" (Teacher reads/tells story)**

_Once upon a time, there was a little person just like you. They had their very own special body - their own hands, feet, tummy, and face. This little person learned that their body belonged to them, and that was very important._

_The little person learned that most touches are good touches - like hugs from mum and dad, holding hands with friends, or when the doctor needs to check if you're healthy. But sometimes touches don't feel right, and that's when it's important to speak up._

_The little person practiced saying "No, I don't like that" in a strong voice. They learned that it's always okay to say no if something doesn't feel right._

### Body Ownership Activity (10 minutes)

**"This is MY Body" Song and Actions**

Teach simple song with actions:

```
🎵 This is my body, it belongs to me (point to self)
From my head down to my toes, you see (touch head, then toes)
I decide who can hug me tight (arms around self)
And I can say no if it's not right (hands up in "stop" gesture)
My body is special, my body is mine (hands on heart)
And keeping it safe feels really fine! 🎵
```

**Reflection Questions:**

- How did it feel to sing about your body being yours?
- What does "your body belongs to you" mean?

## Lesson 2: Good Touch, Not-Good Touch (30 minutes)

### Warm-up Activity (5 minutes)

**"Feelings Faces"**

Show pictures of different facial expressions. Students identify:

- Happy feelings
- Comfortable feelings
- Uncomfortable feelings
- Confused feelings

### Teaching Input (15 minutes)

**Touch Sorting Activity**

Use picture cards or simple drawings to discuss different types of touch:

**Good/Okay Touches:**

- Hugs from family when you want them
- Holding hands with friends
- High-fives
- Doctor checking your ears (with parent there)
- Help getting dressed from family
- Gentle pat on shoulder from teacher

**Not-Good/Not-Okay Touches:**

- Any touch that makes you feel uncomfortable
- Touches on private parts of your body
- Any touch when someone says "don't tell anyone"
- Touches that hurt you
- Any touch you don't want

**Teaching Points:**

- "Most touches are good touches that show care and love"
- "If a touch makes you feel uncomfortable, confused, or scared, it's not okay"
- "You can always say no to touches you don't want"
- "It's never your fault if someone gives you a not-good touch"

### Role-Play Practice (10 minutes)

**"Using My Strong Voice"**

Practice saying NO in different situations:

- Someone wants to tickle you but you don't want to be tickled
- Someone asks for a hug but you don't feel like hugging
- Someone touches your arm and you don't like it

**Practice Phrases:**

- "No, I don't want to"
- "Stop, I don't like that"
- "Please don't touch me"
- "I'm going to tell my teacher/mum/dad"

**Key Message:** "You can always say no to touches you don't want, even from people you know and like."

## Lesson 3: My Trusted Adults (30 minutes)

### Opening Discussion (5 minutes)

**"People Who Care About Me"**

"Who are the grown-ups in your life who love you and keep you safe?"

### Main Activity (20 minutes)

**"My Safety Network" Drawing**

Students draw or use photos to create their personal safety network:

**In the center:** Draw yourself
**Around you:** Draw or paste pictures of trusted adults

**Trusted Adults might include:**

- Mum, Dad, caregivers
- Grandparents
- Teachers
- Family friends
- Neighbours you know well
- Police officers
- School counsellor

**For each trusted adult, discuss:**

- How do you know this person cares about you?
- When might you need to talk to this person?
- How can you contact them or find them?

### Safety Rules Discussion (5 minutes)

**"Our Class Safety Rules"**

Create simple rules together:

1. Always tell a trusted adult if something makes you feel uncomfortable
2. Keep telling until someone listens and helps you
3. It's okay to make a big noise if you need help
4. Never keep scary secrets
5. You are never in trouble for telling a trusted adult about not-good touches

**Class Agreement:** "We will all help each other stay safe and happy."

## Lesson 4: Safe and Unsafe Secrets (25 minutes)

### Story Introduction (10 minutes)

**"The Two Types of Secrets"**

Tell a simple story about two different secrets:

**Happy Secret:** _Jamie's family was planning a surprise birthday party for Grandma. Jamie was so excited to keep this happy secret because it would make Grandma smile and feel loved._

**Scary Secret:** _Someone told Alex to keep a secret that made Alex feel worried and uncomfortable. This secret made Alex's tummy feel funny in a bad way._

### Discussion (10 minutes)

**"Happy Secrets vs. Scary Secrets"**

**Happy Secrets:**

- Make you feel excited and good inside
- Are about nice surprises
- You know you can tell eventually
- Examples: birthday parties, special presents, fun trips

**Scary Secrets:**

- Make you feel worried, scared, or uncomfortable
- Someone tells you "never tell anyone"
- Make your tummy feel bad
- About things that aren't okay

**Important Rule:** "Never keep secrets about touches or things that make you feel scared or uncomfortable."

### Practice Activity (5 minutes)

**"Secret or Tell?"**

Give simple scenarios and students decide "Keep the secret" or "Tell a trusted adult":

- Mum is planning a surprise cake for Dad
- Someone touched you in a way you didn't like and said don't tell
- Your friend is planning to give you a special drawing
- Someone showed you something that made you feel scared

## Lesson 5: Getting Help When I Need It (30 minutes)

### Review Game (10 minutes)

**"Safety Champions Quiz"**

Quick review questions:

- Who does your body belong to? (Me!)
- If someone gives you a not-good touch, what do you do? (Say no and tell a trusted adult)
- Should you keep scary secrets? (No!)
- Who are some of your trusted adults?

### Help-Seeking Strategies (15 minutes)

**"How to Get Help"**

Practice different ways to get help:

**At Home:**

- Find your trusted adult and say "I need to tell you something important"
- If they're busy, say "This can't wait"
- Keep telling until someone listens

**At School:**

- Tell your teacher privately
- Go to the office if your teacher isn't available
- Tell any trusted adult at school

**In the Community:**

- Find a police officer
- Go to a shop and ask them to call your family
- Remember your address and phone number

**Practice Phrases:**

- "I need help please"
- "Something happened that wasn't okay"
- "I need to talk to a grown-up I trust"

### Confidence Building (5 minutes)

**"I Am Brave and Strong"**

Students stand tall and practice saying:

- "I am important"
- "I can ask for help"
- "I can keep myself safe"
- "My feelings matter"
- "Grown-ups should listen to me"

## Assessment Opportunities

### Observation Checklist

Teachers observe and note student understanding:

- [ ] Can identify trusted adults in their life
- [ ] Understands their body belongs to them
- [ ] Can differentiate between good and not-good touches
- [ ] Knows to tell a trusted adult about problems
- [ ] Understands they should never keep scary secrets
- [ ] Can practice saying "no" with confidence

### Simple Verbal Check-ins

**One-on-one conversations with students:**

- "Who would you tell if something made you feel scared?"
- "What would you do if someone wanted to touch you and you didn't want them to?"
- "Can you show me how you would say no in a strong voice?"

**Note:** Any concerning responses should be followed up according to school child protection procedures.

## Differentiation Strategies

### For Students Who Need Extra Support

- **Visual supports:** Picture cards for different emotions and situations
- **One-on-one practice:** Individual time to practice "strong voice"
- **Simplified language:** Use very concrete, simple terms
- **Frequent check-ins:** Regular opportunities to ask questions privately

### For Advanced Students

- **Helper roles:** Support classmates who need encouragement
- **Extended scenarios:** More complex situation discussions
- **Leadership opportunities:** Help demonstrate safe practices

### For Students with Additional Needs

- **Sensory considerations:** Be aware of touch sensitivities
- **Communication adaptations:** Use visual or alternative communication methods
- **Individual plans:** Work with families on personalized safety strategies
