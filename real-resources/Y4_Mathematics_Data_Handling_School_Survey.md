# Year 4 Mathematics: Data Handling Through School Survey

**New Zealand Curriculum Level 3**

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

- Collect, organize, and display data using graphs and charts
- Ask good survey questions and gather reliable information
- Interpret data and draw conclusions
- Present findings clearly to others

---

## Lesson Sequence (5 lessons)

### Lesson 1: Planning Our Survey

#### Hook Activity (10 minutes)

**"What Do You Want to Know?"**

Show students interesting questions about their school:

- How many students walk to school vs. get driven?
- What's the most popular lunch food in our class?
- Which playground equipment do students use most?
- What sports do Year 4 students like best?

Ask: "How could we find answers to these questions?"

#### Input (15 minutes)

**Good Survey Questions**

**Examples of GOOD survey questions:**

- "How do you get to school?" (walk, car, bus, bike, scooter)
- "What is your favorite subject?" (maths, reading, science, art, PE, social studies)
- "How many pets do you have?" (0, 1, 2, 3, 4 or more)

**Examples of POOR survey questions:**

- "Do you like school?" (too general)
- "What did you have for breakfast today?" (too many possible answers)
- "Are you good at maths?" (opinion, hard to measure)

**Characteristics of Good Survey Questions:**

1. **Clear and specific**
2. **Limited answer options**
3. **Not too personal**
4. **Interesting to most people**
5. **Possible to answer accurately**

#### Group Planning (20 minutes)

**Choose Your Survey Topic**

Divide class into groups of 4. Each group chooses a topic:

**Topic Options:**

1. **Transportation to School**
2. **Favorite School Subjects**
3. **Lunch Preferences**
4. **After-School Activities**
5. **Favorite Books/Reading**
6. **Weekend Activities**

**Planning Sheet for Groups:**

```
Our Topic: _________________________________

Our Survey Question: _______________________
_________________________________________

Answer Options:
1. ____________________________________
2. ____________________________________
3. ____________________________________
4. ____________________________________
5. ____________________________________

Who will we survey? _______________________
(Our class? Year 4? Whole school?)

How many people do we want to ask? __________
```

#### Plenary (5 minutes)

Groups share their survey question with the class for feedback.

---

### Lesson 2: Collecting Data

#### Warm-up (5 minutes)

**Tally Practice**
Practice making tally marks for numbers: 3, 8, 12, 17, 23

#### Data Collection (35 minutes)

**Survey Time!**

Groups go out and conduct their surveys using tally charts:

**Sample Data Collection Sheet:**

```
Survey Question: How do you get to school?

Walk        |  Car         |  Bus         |  Bike        |  Scooter
||||  ||    |  |||| ||||   |  |||         |  ||          |  |
(7)         |  (9)         |  (3)         |  (2)         |  (1)
```

**Survey Rules:**

1. Be polite and friendly
2. Ask permission before surveying other classes
3. Record answers accurately with tally marks
4. Thank people for their time
5. Work together as a team

**Teacher circulates to help with:**

- Clear question asking
- Accurate tally recording
- Managing group dynamics
- Encouraging shy students

#### Data Check (10 minutes)

Groups return and count their tallies, converting to numbers.

**Quality Check Questions:**

- Did you ask your question clearly?
- Are your tallies accurate?
- How many people did you survey in total?
- Were there any problems or surprises?

---

### Lesson 3: Organizing and Displaying Data

#### Starter (5 minutes)

**Data Detective**
Show a simple bar graph and ask students what information they can find.

#### Input (15 minutes)

**Types of Graphs and When to Use Them**

**Pictographs:** Good for small amounts of data, easy to understand

- Use symbols to represent data
- Each symbol = 1 person (or more for larger numbers)

**Bar Graphs:** Good for comparing different categories

- Bars show the amount for each category
- Easy to see which category has the most/least

**Pie Charts:** Good for showing parts of a whole

- Shows what fraction/percentage each part represents
- Circle divided into sections

#### Guided Practice (25 minutes)

**Create Your Graph**

Each group creates TWO different types of graphs with their data:

**Step 1: Pictograph (15 minutes)**

```
How Year 4 Students Get to School

Walk    😊😊😊😊😊😊😊 (7 students)
Car     😊😊😊😊😊😊😊😊😊 (9 students)
Bus     😊😊😊 (3 students)
Bike    😊😊 (2 students)
Scooter 😊 (1 student)

Each 😊 = 1 student
```

**Step 2: Bar Graph (10 minutes)**
Using graph paper or pre-made templates, groups create bar graphs.

**Graph Requirements:**

- Title describing what the graph shows
- Labels on both axes
- Equal spacing between bars
- Numbers marked clearly on the vertical axis

#### Plenary (5 minutes)

**Graph Gallery Walk**
Groups display their pictographs around the room for others to view.

---

### Lesson 4: Interpreting Data and Drawing Conclusions

#### Hook (5 minutes)

**Data Questions Rapid Fire**
Using completed graphs, ask quick questions:

- Which category has the most?
- How many more students chose X than Y?
- What is the total number surveyed?

#### Analysis Activity (30 minutes)

**Becoming Data Detectives**

Groups analyze their own and other groups' data:

**Analysis Questions for Own Data:**

1. What was the most popular answer? How many people chose it?
2. What was the least popular answer? How many people chose it?
3. What is the difference between the highest and lowest numbers?
4. How many people did you survey altogether?
5. If you surveyed the whole school, what do you predict would happen?

**Analysis Questions for Other Groups' Data:**

1. What surprises you about this data?
2. What questions do you have about how they collected this information?
3. How does this compare to what you expected?
4. What might explain these results?

**Recording Conclusions:**

```
Our Survey Results Show:

The most important finding is: ________________
____________________________________________

This surprised us: ___________________________
____________________________________________

We think this happened because: _______________
____________________________________________

If we did this survey again, we would: ________
____________________________________________
```

#### Comparison Activity (10 minutes)

**Cross-Group Analysis**

Groups rotate to examine each other's graphs and data:

- What patterns do you notice?
- How reliable do you think this data is?
- What other questions does this data make you think of?

#### Plenary (5 minutes)

**One Big Learning**
Each group shares one important thing they learned from analyzing data.

---

### Lesson 5: Presenting Findings

#### Preparation (20 minutes)

**Create a Data Presentation**

Groups prepare a 3-minute presentation including:

**Presentation Structure:**

1. **Introduction:** "We wanted to find out..."
2. **Method:** "We surveyed \_\_\_ people by asking..."
3. **Results:** "Here's what we found..." (show graphs)
4. **Analysis:** "The most interesting result was..."
5. **Conclusion:** "This tells us that..."

**Presentation Materials:**

- Large version of their best graph
- List of key findings
- One interesting conclusion or recommendation

#### Presentations (25 minutes)

**Data Fair**

Each group presents their findings (3 minutes each + 2 minutes for questions).

**Audience Guidelines:**

- Listen respectfully
- Ask one thoughtful question
- Give positive feedback about something interesting

**Assessment Focus:**

- Clear explanation of data collection method
- Accurate interpretation of graphs
- Reasonable conclusions based on evidence
- Good presentation skills

#### Reflection (5 minutes)

**Survey Scientist Reflection**

Individual reflection on learning:

```

_________________________________________________

_________________________________________________

_________________________________________________
```

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment

- **Observation during data collection** - accuracy and teamwork
- **Graph creation process** - understanding of different graph types
- **Analysis discussions** - quality of conclusions drawn
- **Peer feedback** during presentations

### Summative Assessment Rubric

| Criteria                    | Excellent (4)                                            | Good (3)                                   | Satisfactory (2)                             | Needs Work (1)                             |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Data Collection**         | Systematic, accurate recording with clear method         | Good recording with mostly accurate data   | Basic recording with some inaccuracies       | Poor recording, unreliable data            |
| **Graph Creation**          | Graphs are clear, accurate, well-labeled                 | Graphs are mostly clear and accurate       | Graphs are basic but readable                | Graphs are unclear or inaccurate           |
| **Data Analysis**           | Sophisticated interpretation with reasonable conclusions | Good interpretation with clear conclusions | Basic interpretation with simple conclusions | Little interpretation, unclear conclusions |
| **Presentation**            | Confident, clear presentation with audience engagement   | Good presentation with clear communication | Basic presentation, mostly clear             | Unclear presentation, hard to follow       |
| **Mathematical Vocabulary** | Uses data terminology accurately throughout              | Uses most data terms correctly             | Uses some data vocabulary appropriately      | Limited use of mathematical language       |

## Extension Activities

### For Advanced Students

- **Compare surveys** from different classes or year levels
- **Calculate percentages** for each category
- **Predict future trends** based on current data
- **Design follow-up surveys** to investigate surprising results

### For Students Needing Support

- **Use concrete materials** (blocks, counters) to represent data
- **Partner with stronger student** for data analysis
- **Focus on one type of graph** rather than multiple formats
- **Provide sentence starters** for conclusions

## Teacher Notes

### Management Tips

- **Group roles:** Assign specific jobs (questioner, recorder, time-keeper, encourager)
- **Survey ethics:** Discuss respectful questioning and accepting "no" answers
- **Data integrity:** Emphasize honest, accurate recording
- **Time management:** Use timers for each activity phase

### Common Challenges

- **Students asking leading questions:** Practice neutral question asking
- **Inaccurate tally counting:** Regular check-ins during collection
- **Overwhelming data:** Help groups focus on main patterns
- **Presentation nerves:** Practice and peer support

### Assessment Notes

- **Focus on process** as much as final product
- **Celebrate interesting questions** and creative approaches
- **Document learning progression** through photos and work samples
- **Connect to real-world** data use in students' lives

This unit develops statistical literacy while engaging students in authentic inquiry about their school community, building foundation skills for more complex data analysis in future years.
