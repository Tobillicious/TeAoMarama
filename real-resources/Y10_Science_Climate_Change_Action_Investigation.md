# Year 10 Science: Climate Change Action Investigation

**New Zealand Curriculum Level 5**

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

- Understand the scientific evidence for climate change and its causes
- Investigate local impacts of climate change in New Zealand contexts
- Evaluate different solutions and mitigation strategies
- Design and conduct scientific investigations related to environmental change
- Communicate findings effectively to diverse audiences

---

## Unit Overview (12 lessons + field work)

This unit combines rigorous scientific investigation with local environmental action, enabling students to understand climate science while developing practical solutions for their community.

---

## Lesson 1: Climate vs Weather - Understanding the Science

### Hook Activity (15 minutes)

**"Climate Detective Challenge"**

Students analyze data sets:

- Daily weather records from their town for past month
- Average temperature data for their region over past 50 years
- Global temperature anomaly graphs
- Local sea level measurements

**Questions to investigate:**

- What patterns do you notice in short-term vs long-term data?
- How do local changes compare to global trends?
- What evidence would convince a skeptical friend that climate is changing?

### Scientific Foundation (25 minutes)

**"The Physics of Climate Change"**

**Greenhouse Effect Investigation:**

- Model using clear containers, thermometers, and heat lamps
- Compare temperatures with and without "greenhouse gases" (clear plastic wrap)
- Measure and graph temperature changes over time
- Connect to molecular-level understanding of infrared radiation absorption

**Key Concepts:**

- **Electromagnetic spectrum** and energy absorption
- **Carbon cycle** and human perturbations
- **Feedback loops** (positive and negative)
- **Radiative forcing** and energy balance

**New Zealand Context:**

- How does our geography affect climate patterns?
- What are our major sources of greenhouse gas emissions?
- How do ocean currents influence our climate?

### Data Analysis Activity (15 minutes)

**"Reading the Evidence"**

Students examine multiple data sources:

- NIWA temperature records for New Zealand
- Atmospheric CO2 measurements from Baring Head
- Glacier retreat photos from Southern Alps
- Sea level rise data from coastal monitoring stations

**Analysis Skills:**

- Identifying trends and patterns in data
- Distinguishing correlation from causation
- Understanding uncertainty and error bars
- Evaluating data quality and sources

## Lesson 2: Local Climate Impacts Investigation

### Field Preparation (10 minutes)

**"Becoming Environmental Scientists"**

**Investigation Question:** How is climate change affecting our local environment?

**Field Sites Options:**

- Local beach or coastal area (sea level, erosion)
- Native forest remnant (species distribution, phenology)
- Agricultural area (growing seasons, crop yields)
- Urban environment (heat islands, stormwater)

### Field Investigation (60 minutes)

**"Gathering Primary Evidence"**

**Data Collection Methods:**

1. **Temperature Monitoring:** Use data loggers in different microclimates
2. **Species Surveys:** Document plant/animal species and behavior
3. **Physical Measurements:** Erosion, water quality, soil temperature
4. **Photography:** Visual evidence with GPS coordinates
5. **Interviews:** Local knowledge from farmers, gardeners, iwi

**Scientific Method Application:**

- Formulate specific hypotheses about local changes
- Design controlled observations where possible
- Record quantitative and qualitative data
- Consider variables and potential confounding factors

### Interview Component (15 minutes)

**"Local Knowledge Integration"**

Students interview community members:

- **Kaumātua and iwi members:** Traditional ecological knowledge
- **Farmers and growers:** Agricultural observations
- **Long-term residents:** Changes they've witnessed
- **Local council staff:** Infrastructure and planning concerns

**Key Questions:**

- What changes have you noticed in [seasons/weather/plants/animals]?
- When did you first notice these changes?
- How have these changes affected [your work/community/environment]?
- What adaptations have you made?

## Lesson 3: Data Analysis and Pattern Recognition

### Data Processing Workshop (20 minutes)

**"Making Sense of Our Findings"**

Students work in investigation teams to:

- Organize and clean collected data
- Create graphs and visual representations
- Calculate basic statistics (means, trends, ranges)
- Compare their findings to published research

**Technology Integration:**

- Spreadsheet analysis of numerical data
- GIS mapping of spatial data
- Digital presentation of photographic evidence
- Statistical software for trend analysis

### Pattern Analysis (20 minutes)

**"What Story Does the Data Tell?"**

**Guided Analysis Questions:**

1. What trends or patterns do you observe in your data?
2. How do your findings compare to regional/national trends?
3. What could explain the patterns you're seeing?
4. What questions does your data raise?
5. How confident are you in your conclusions?

**Scientific Reasoning:**

- Distinguishing between evidence and interpretation
- Considering alternative explanations
- Acknowledging limitations and uncertainties
- Making predictions based on trends

### Research Connection (10 minutes)

**"Placing Our Work in Context"**

Students research published studies:

- Find peer-reviewed research on similar topics
- Compare their methods and findings to professional studies
- Identify gaps their investigation might help fill
- Consider how local data contributes to global understanding

## Lesson 4: Climate Solutions Investigation

### Solutions Framework (15 minutes)

**"Mitigation vs Adaptation Strategies"**

**Mitigation:** Reducing greenhouse gas emissions

- Renewable energy transitions
- Transportation changes
- Industrial process improvements
- Carbon sequestration methods

**Adaptation:** Adjusting to climate change impacts

- Infrastructure modifications
- Agricultural adaptations
- Ecosystem management
- Emergency preparedness

### New Zealand Solutions Analysis (25 minutes)

**"What Can Aotearoa Do?"**

Students research and evaluate:

**Energy Solutions:**

- Geothermal, wind, and solar potential
- Electric vehicle adoption
- Energy efficiency in buildings
- Green hydrogen development

**Agricultural Solutions:**

- Methane reduction from livestock
- Regenerative farming practices
- Carbon farming and forestry
- Climate-resilient crop varieties

**Natural Solutions:**

- Wetland restoration for carbon storage
- Native forest regeneration
- Blue carbon in coastal ecosystems
- Biodiversity conservation strategies

**Social and Economic Solutions:**

- Carbon pricing mechanisms
- Just transition for workers
- Community resilience building
- Indigenous knowledge integration

### Solution Evaluation Matrix (10 minutes)

**"Comparing Effectiveness"**

Students create evaluation criteria:

- **Feasibility:** How realistic is implementation?
- **Impact:** How much difference would it make?
- **Cost:** What are the economic considerations?
- **Co-benefits:** What other advantages exist?
- **Equity:** Who benefits and who bears costs?
- **Timeline:** How quickly could it be implemented?

## Lesson 5: Design Thinking for Climate Action

### Problem Definition (15 minutes)

**"Focusing Our Efforts"**

Based on their local investigation, students identify specific problems:

- What local climate impacts need addressing?
- Who is most affected by these changes?
- What resources and constraints exist?
- What solutions haven't been tried locally?

### Solution Design Workshop (30 minutes)

**"Innovation Through Collaboration"**

**Design Thinking Process:**

1. **Empathize:** Who are we designing for?
2. **Define:** What is the core problem?
3. **Ideate:** What are all possible solutions?
4. **Prototype:** How can we test ideas quickly?
5. **Test:** What works and what doesn't?

**Solution Categories:**

- **Technology solutions:** Apps, monitoring systems, efficiency tools
- **Behavior change initiatives:** Education, incentives, social norms
- **Policy proposals:** Regulations, funding, institutional changes
- **Community actions:** Local projects, collaboration networks
- **Natural solutions:** Ecosystem restoration, green infrastructure

### Prototype Development (5 minutes)

**"Making Ideas Tangible"**

Students choose one solution concept to develop:

- Create visual mockups or models
- Map out implementation steps
- Identify required resources and partnerships
- Plan pilot testing or demonstration

## Lesson 6: Scientific Communication and Advocacy

### Audience Analysis (10 minutes)

**"Who Needs to Hear This?"**

Students identify key audiences for their findings:

- **School community:** Students, teachers, families
- **Local government:** Council members, planning staff
- **Community groups:** Environmental organizations, service clubs
- **Media:** Local newspapers, radio, social media
- **Policymakers:** MPs, regional council representatives

### Communication Strategy Development (25 minutes)

**"Matching Message to Medium"**

**For Scientific Community:**

- Formal research reports with methodology and data
- Peer review process with classmates
- Conference-style presentations with Q&A
- Data visualizations and statistical analysis

**For General Public:**

- Infographics and visual storytelling
- Social media campaigns with clear calls to action
- Community presentation with local examples
- Interactive demonstrations and models

**For Policymakers:**

- Policy briefs with recommendations
- Economic analysis of costs and benefits
- Stakeholder impact assessments
- Clear action steps and timelines

### Message Development (15 minutes)

**"Crafting Compelling Arguments"**

**Key Components:**

1. **Hook:** Why should audience care?
2. **Evidence:** What does the science show?
3. **Local relevance:** How does this affect us here?
4. **Solutions:** What can be done about it?
5. **Call to action:** What specific steps can audience take?

**Scientific Integrity:**

- Accurate representation of uncertainty
- Clear distinction between facts and opinions
- Acknowledgment of limitations
- Ethical considerations in communication

## Lesson 7-8: Solution Implementation Project

### Project Planning (30 minutes)

**"From Idea to Action"**

Students develop detailed implementation plans:

**Project Components:**

- **Problem statement:** Clear definition of issue being addressed
- **Solution description:** Detailed explanation of proposed intervention
- **Evidence base:** Scientific justification for approach
- **Implementation plan:** Step-by-step action timeline
- **Resource requirements:** Materials, funding, partnerships needed
- **Success metrics:** How will effectiveness be measured?
- **Risk assessment:** What could go wrong and how to mitigate?

### Community Engagement (40 minutes)

**"Building Support and Partnerships"**

**Stakeholder Mapping:**

- Identify all parties affected by or needed for solution
- Analyze their interests, concerns, and potential contributions
- Develop engagement strategies for each group
- Plan communication and collaboration approaches

**Partnership Development:**

- Reach out to local organizations and experts
- Propose collaboration or mentorship relationships
- Seek feedback on solution feasibility and design
- Identify resources and support available

### Pilot Implementation (20 minutes)

**"Testing Our Approach"**

Where feasible, students implement small-scale pilots:

- **School-based projects:** Energy audits, waste reduction, transportation surveys
- **Digital solutions:** Apps, websites, social media campaigns
- **Community education:** Presentations, information materials, demonstrations
- **Data collection:** Ongoing monitoring of local environmental indicators

---

## Lesson 9: Evaluation and Iteration

### Results Analysis (20 minutes)

**"What Did We Learn?"**

Students evaluate their pilot projects:

- **Quantitative results:** Measured changes, participation rates, resource use
- **Qualitative feedback:** Stakeholder responses, unexpected outcomes, lessons learned
- **Process evaluation:** What worked well in implementation? What was challenging?
- **Impact assessment:** Did the solution address the intended problem?

### Iteration and Improvement (20 minutes)

**"Making It Better"**

Based on evaluation results:

- Identify specific improvements to solution design
- Modify implementation strategies based on lessons learned
- Incorporate stakeholder feedback into revised plans
- Develop scaling strategies for broader implementation

### Scientific Reflection (10 minutes)

**"Understanding the Research Process"**

Students reflect on their experience as researchers:

- How did their understanding of climate science evolve?
- What surprised them about the research process?
- How does uncertainty affect decision-making?
- What skills did they develop through this investigation?

## Lesson 10: Policy and Systems Thinking

### Policy Analysis Workshop (25 minutes)

**"How Do Systems Change?"**

Students analyze existing climate policies:

- **National level:** New Zealand's emissions reduction targets, carbon pricing
- **Regional level:** Climate adaptation plans, transportation policies
- **Local level:** Building codes, renewable energy incentives, land use planning

**Analysis Framework:**

- What problem is the policy trying to solve?
- What mechanisms does it use to create change?
- Who are the winners and losers?
- How effective has it been so far?
- What improvements could be made?

### Systems Mapping (20 minutes)

**"Understanding Interconnections"**

Students create visual maps showing:

- **Cause and effect relationships** in climate and human systems
- **Feedback loops** that amplify or dampen changes
- **Leverage points** where small changes could have big impacts
- **Stakeholder relationships** and power dynamics
- **Multiple scales** from individual to global

### Policy Proposal Development (5 minutes)

**"Recommendations for Change"**

Based on their investigation and analysis, students develop policy recommendations:

- Specific, actionable proposals
- Clear rationale based on evidence
- Consideration of implementation challenges
- Stakeholder impact analysis

---

## Lesson 11-12: Conference Presentation and Action Planning

### Scientific Conference Simulation (40 minutes)

**"Sharing Knowledge with the Scientific Community"**

Students present their research in academic conference format:

- **Oral presentations:** 10 minutes + 5 minutes questions
- **Poster sessions:** Visual displays with informal discussion
- **Panel discussions:** Multiple perspectives on common themes
- **Peer review:** Constructive feedback using scientific criteria

**Presentation Requirements:**

- Clear research question and methodology
- Data presentation with appropriate visualizations
- Discussion of limitations and uncertainties
- Implications for further research
- Practical applications of findings

### Community Action Fair (40 minutes)

**"Inspiring Community Engagement"**

Students present solutions to authentic community audience:

- **Solution showcases:** Interactive demonstrations of proposed interventions
- **Call to action:** Specific ways audience members can get involved
- **Partnership opportunities:** Ways community can support student projects
- **Resource sharing:** Information and tools for audience use

### Commitment and Next Steps (10 minutes)

**"Continuing the Work"**

Students make personal and collective commitments:

- Individual actions they will take based on their learning
- Ongoing monitoring or research they will continue
- Community partnerships they will maintain
- Skills they want to develop further

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment Throughout Unit

- **Daily science journal** reflections and observations
- **Peer review** of data collection and analysis methods
- **Progress check-ins** on investigation and solution development
- **Stakeholder feedback** on communication and engagement efforts

### Summative Assessment Portfolio (100 marks)

**1. Scientific Investigation Report (40 marks)**

- Clear research question and methodology (10 marks)
- Accurate data collection and analysis (15 marks)
- Evidence-based conclusions with uncertainty acknowledgment (10 marks)
- Connection to broader climate science understanding (5 marks)

**2. Solution Design and Implementation (30 marks)**

- Problem definition and solution appropriateness (10 marks)
- Feasibility analysis and evidence base (10 marks)
- Implementation planning and stakeholder engagement (10 marks)

**3. Communication and Advocacy (20 marks)**

- Clarity and accuracy of scientific communication (10 marks)
- Audience appropriateness and engagement effectiveness (10 marks)

**4. Reflection and Systems Thinking (10 marks)**

- Understanding of climate science complexity (5 marks)
- Recognition of policy and systems challenges (5 marks)

### Peer Assessment Component

- **Collaboration effectiveness** during group work and field investigations
- **Constructive feedback** provided to classmates during peer review
- **Support and encouragement** shown to team members throughout unit
