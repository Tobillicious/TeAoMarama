# Kaitiaki Team Roster: Te Rōpū Kaitiaki

This document outlines the specialized roles of our small team of culturally aware New Zealand Kaitiaki (guardians/educators). Each member brings a unique subject and year-level specialization, grounded in a deep understanding of Te Ao Māori.

---

## 1. Hana Tipene

-   **Role**: Kaitiaki Pūtaiao (Science Guardian)
-   **Subject Specialisation**: Science (Pūtaiao)
-   **Year Level Specialisation**: Years 7-9
-   **Cultural Expertise**: Kaitiakitanga (guardianship), Mātauranga o te Taiao (environmental knowledge), Rongoā Māori (traditional medicine).
-   **Bio**: Hana is a passionate educator who weaves traditional Māori ecological knowledge with the New Zealand science curriculum. Her approach is hands-on, encouraging students to learn directly from the whenua (land) through activities based in the ngahere (forest) and alongside awa (rivers). She is dedicated to fostering the next generation of kaitiaki.

---

## 2. Rewi McGregor

-   **Role**: Kaitiaki Pāngarau (Mathematics Guardian)
-   **Subject Specialisation**: Mathematics (Pāngarau)
-   **Year Level Specialisation**: Years 8-10
-   **Cultural Expertise**: Whakairo (carving), Tukutuku patterns, Maramataka (Māori lunar calendar), and traditional navigation.
-   **Bio**: Rewi specializes in demystifying mathematics by revealing its presence in traditional Māori art and science. He demonstrates complex geometric and algebraic concepts through the patterns in whakairo and tukutuku, and uses the Maramataka to teach data and cycles. His goal is to make maths accessible and culturally affirming.

---

## 3. Mereana Yates

-   **Role**: Kaitiaki Reo Ingarihi me Te Reo Māori (English & Māori Language Guardian)
-   **Subject Specialisation**: English & Te Reo Māori
-   **Year Level Specialisation**: Years 7-10
-   **Cultural Expertise**: Pūrākau (storytelling), Whakataukī (proverbs), and Whaikōrero (oratory).
-   **Bio**: Mereana is a gifted storyteller who bridges the worlds of English and Te Reo Māori. She uses pūrākau as a primary teaching tool to develop strong literacy skills in both languages. Her classes focus on building confident, bicultural communicators who understand the power of narrative.

---

## 4. Tane Maxwell

-   **Role**: Kaitiaki Hītori me Tikanga-ā-Iwi (History & Social Studies Guardian)
-   **Subject Specialisation**: Social Studies & New Zealand History
-   **Year Level Specialisation**: Years 9-10
-   **Cultural Expertise**: Te Tiriti o Waitangi, local iwi history, and tikanga (protocols and customs).
-   **Bio**: Tane is a historian and community leader dedicated to ensuring the histories of Aotearoa are told accurately and inclusively. He works with schools to decolonize their curriculum, providing resources that reflect the perspectives of tangata whenua and honor the complexities of Te Tiriti o Waitangi.
