
# Lesson Plan: Enhancing Sentences with Appositives

**Class:** Year 8  
**Learning Area:** Structured Literacy  
**Angawā:** Period 3  
**Course:** TMC | Structured Literacy Term 3  
**Kaiako:** [Teacher Name]  

**MANGAKŌTUKUTUKUTANGA COLLEGE LOCALISED CURRICULUM**

**Lesson title:** Whakarākai Kōrero - Decorating Sentences with Appositives  
**Learning Intention:** Students will use appositives to add specific details and descriptions to their sentences, drawing on cultural contexts.  
**Achievement Objective:** Construct clear, coherent sentences using varied patterns and appropriate language features.

**Prior knowledge:** Basic sentence expansion (who, what, when, where, why, how).  
**Success Criteria:** Students can correctly identify and create sentences with appositives that add meaningful, culturally relevant information.

**Keywords/Glossary:** 
- Appositive (a noun or noun phrase that renames another noun right beside it)
- Whakarākai (to decorate, to adorn)
- Detail (a specific piece of information)

## LESSON OUTLINE

**TIME (45 mins)** | **LEARNING EXPERIENCE** | **RESOURCES**

### DO NOW (10 mins)
- **Cultural Connection:** Display a picture of a well-known marae. Ask students to write down one sentence describing it.
- **Review:** Share a few sentences and ask students to expand them using the "who, what, when, where, why, how" method.
- **Engagement:** Show two sentences: "Tāne Mahuta is a giant kauri tree. Tāne Mahuta is a symbol of strength." Ask students how they could combine them to be more effective.

**Resources:** Image of a marae, whiteboard, markers.

### LESSON BODY (50 mins)

**Introduction to Appositives (15 mins)**
- Explain that an appositive is a powerful tool to add detail, acting like a descriptive rename.
- Use the "Do Now" example to model combining the sentences: "Tāne Mahuta, a giant kauri tree, is a symbol of strength."
- Provide a clear structure: Noun, **appositive phrase**, rest of the sentence.
- Share another cultural example: "The korowai, a traditional Māori cloak, was woven with great care."

**Guided Practice (15 mins)**
- Work through examples as a class, combining kernel sentences into single, richer sentences using appositives.
  1.  Kernel 1: Aoraki is the highest mountain in New Zealand.
      Kernel 2: Aoraki is a sacred ancestor.
      Combined: Aoraki, the highest mountain in New Zealand, is a sacred ancestor.
  2.  Kernel 1: The haka is a traditional Māori dance.
      Kernel 2: The haka is performed with passion and energy.
      Combined: The haka, a traditional Māori dance, is performed with passion and energy.

**Independent Practice (15 mins)**
- Students work in pairs to create their own sentences with appositives based on prompts.
  - Prompt 1: Describe a taonga (treasure) that is important to you or your whānau.
  - Prompt 2: Write about a significant place in your local area.
  - Prompt 3: Describe a kai (food) that is special to a celebration.
- Encourage the use of rich, descriptive language.

**Cultural Integration (5 mins)**
- Discuss how appositives can be used to share cultural knowledge efficiently and beautifully within a single sentence.
- Share and celebrate the culturally rich sentences created by the students.

**Resources:** Appositive sentence templates, list of cultural nouns (e.g., pounamu, Tāne Mahuta, Hangi), student notebooks.

### Success Criteria (15 mins)
- **Student Demonstration:** Students share their appositive sentences with a partner or the class.
- **Formative Assessment:** Teacher reviews student sentences for correct structure and meaningful detail.
- **Cultural Knowledge Application:** Students effectively use appositives to convey cultural information.
- **Next Steps Preparation:** Challenge students to find examples of appositives in their independent reading.

**Resources:** Student work, peer feedback checklist.

## "Plan B" (Technology Failure Alternatives)
- **Sentence Matching Game:** Have sentence kernels and appositive phrases on separate cards for students to match.
- **Gallery Walk:** Write kernel sentences on large sheets of paper around the room. Students circulate and add appositive phrases.
- **Oral Practice:** Students create and share appositive sentences verbally in a circle.

## Filler Activities (Five minute activities for early finishers)
- **Appositive Hunt:** Look for examples of appositives in a provided text (e.g., a news article about a cultural event).
- **Sentence Challenge:** See who can write the longest, grammatically correct sentence using an appositive.
- **Vocabulary Enrichment:** Brainstorm powerful adjectives to use within appositive phrases.

## Lesson Reflection
**What worked well:**
- The concept of "renaming" was an effective way to explain appositives.
- The cultural prompts for independent practice led to rich and personal sentences.
- Students enjoyed the challenge of combining ideas into a single, elegant sentence.

**Areas for improvement:**
- Some students may need more practice with the correct comma usage around appositives.
- Provide a wider range of cultural nouns as prompts.
- Connect this lesson more explicitly to the multi-paragraph writing goals.

**Student engagement:**
- High engagement, particularly during the independent practice with cultural prompts.
- Students showed pride in the sophisticated sentences they were able to construct.

## Next Steps
- **Follow-up Lesson:** Introduce other sentence patterns, such as clauses, to build even more complex sentences.
- **Homework:** Find three examples of appositives in their reading and write three of their own about their hobbies or interests.
- **Assessment:** Include a section on the next writing assessment that specifically scores the use of appositives for descriptive detail.

## Cultural Integration Notes
This lesson empowers students to weave cultural knowledge directly into the structure of their writing. By using appositives, they can share important details about their identity, heritage, and environment in a way that is both informative and grammatically sophisticated.
