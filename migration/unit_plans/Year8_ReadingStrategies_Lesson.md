
# Lesson Plan: Reading Comprehension with a Pūrākau

**Class:** Year 8  
**Learning Area:** Structured Literacy  
**Angawā:** Period 2  
**Course:** TMC | Structured Literacy Term 3  
**Kaiako:** [Teacher Name]  

**MANGAKŌTUKUTUKU COLLEGE LOCALISED CURRICULUM**

**Lesson title:** Te Māramatanga ki te Pūrākau - Understanding a Traditional Māori Story  
**Learning Intention:** Students will apply reading comprehension strategies to understand and analyze a pūrākau (traditional Māori story), focusing on identifying the main idea, making inferences, and understanding vocabulary in context.  
**Achievement Objective:** Make sense of increasingly complex, varied, and challenging texts.

**Prior knowledge:** Familiarity with pūrākau, basic reading comprehension skills.  
**Success Criteria:** Students can identify the main idea of the pūrākau, make at least two reasonable inferences based on the text, and define key vocabulary words from the context.

**Keywords/Glossary:** 
- Pūrākau (traditional Māori story)
- Main Idea (the central point of the text)
- Inference (a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning)
- Context Clues (hints in the text that help define a word)

## LESSON OUTLINE

**TIME (45 mins)** | **LEARNING EXPERIENCE** | **RESOURCES**

### DO NOW (10 mins)
- **Cultural Connection:** Brainstorm what students know about Māui and his exploits. Create a word cloud on the whiteboard.
- **Review:** Briefly recap the concept of a "main idea" from previous lessons.
- **Engagement:** Show an image depicting one of Māui's stories and ask students to predict what the story might be about.

**Resources:** Whiteboard, markers, image of Māui.

### LESSON BODY (50 mins)

**Introduction to the Pūrākau (10 mins)**
- Introduce the story of "Māui and the Sun" (Te Hopu a Māui i a Te Rā).
- Provide a brief summary and explain its cultural significance.
- Distribute a short, accessible version of the pūrākau.

**Shared Reading (15 mins)**
- Read the pūrākau aloud as a class.
- Pause at key points to ask questions and check for understanding.
- Model how to use context clues to understand unfamiliar words (e.g., "Tama-nui-te-rā" - the great son of the sun).

**Guided Practice: Comprehension Strategies (20 mins)**
- **Main Idea:** In small groups, students discuss and determine the main idea of the story.
- **Inference:** As a class, identify parts of the text that require inference (e.g., "Why did Māui's brothers doubt him?").
- **Vocabulary in Context:** Students work in pairs to define key vocabulary from the text using context clues.

**Cultural Integration (5 mins)**
- Discuss the values and lessons embedded in the pūrākau (e.g., courage, ingenuity, the importance of working together).
- Connect the story to the students' own experiences and understanding of the world.

**Resources:** Copies of the "Māui and the Sun" pūrākau, graphic organizers for main idea and inference, vocabulary worksheets.

### Success Criteria (15 mins)
- **Student Demonstration:** Students share their findings for the main idea, inferences, and vocabulary.
- **Formative Assessment:** Teacher circulates and observes group discussions, providing feedback.
- **Cultural Knowledge Application:** Students explain the cultural lessons from the pūrākau.
- **Next Steps Preparation:** Introduce the idea of comparing different versions of the same pūrākau.

**Resources:** Student work, assessment checklist.

## "Plan B" (Technology Failure Alternatives)
- **Oral Storytelling:** Tell the pūrākau orally and have students create visual representations of the story.
- **Dramatic Interpretation:** Students act out scenes from the pūrākau.
- **Shared Writing:** As a class, write a summary of the pūrākau on the whiteboard.

## Filler Activities (Five minute activities for early finishers)
- **Creative Writing:** Write a short story from the perspective of one of Māui's brothers.
- **Art:** Draw a scene from the pūrākau.
- **Research:** Use books or other resources to find out more about Māui.

## Lesson Reflection
**What worked well:**
- The pūrākau was highly engaging for students.
- The comprehension strategies were well-understood and applied.
- The cultural connection deepened students' understanding of the text.

**Areas for improvement:**
- Provide more scaffolding for students who struggle with inference.
- Differentiate the text for varying reading levels.
- Incorporate more te reo Māori into the lesson.

**Student engagement:**
- High level of engagement and participation.
- Students were eager to share their own knowledge of Māui.
- The group work fostered collaboration and discussion.

## Next Steps
- **Follow-up Lesson:** Compare and contrast different versions of the "Māui and the Sun" pūrākau.
- **Homework:** Read another pūrākau and identify the main idea, make inferences, and define vocabulary.
- **Assessment:** A written response to the pūrākau, analyzing its themes and characters.

## Cultural Integration Notes
This lesson uses a culturally significant pūrākau to teach reading comprehension skills, allowing students to connect with their heritage while developing their literacy. The focus on the values and lessons within the story reinforces the importance of mātauranga Māori.
