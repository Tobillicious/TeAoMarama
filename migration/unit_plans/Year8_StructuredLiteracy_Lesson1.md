# Lesson Plan: Sentence Expansion with Cultural Context

**Class:** Year 8  
**Learning Area:** Structured Literacy  
**Angawā:** Period 1  
**Course:** TMC | Structured Literacy Term 3  
**Kaiako:** [Teacher Name]  

**MANGAKŌTUKUTUKU COLLEGE LOCALISED CURRICULUM**

**Lesson title:** Whakawhānui i ngā Kōrero - Expanding Sentences with Cultural Knowledge  
**Learning Intention:** Students will expand basic sentences by adding details about who, what, when, where, why, and how, using cultural contexts and te reo Māori vocabulary.  
**Achievement Objective:** Construct clear, coherent sentences using varied patterns and appropriate language features.

**Prior knowledge:** Basic sentence structure (subject + verb), familiarity with te reo Māori vocabulary, understanding of cultural concepts like whakapapa and mana.  
**Success Criteria:** Students can transform a basic kernel sentence into a detailed, informative sentence that includes cultural context and answers multiple question words.

**Keywords/Glossary:** 
- Whakawhānui (expansion)
- Kōrero (speech, talk)
- Kernel sentence (basic sentence structure)
- Appositive (descriptive phrase)
- Cultural context (connection to Māori knowledge and values)

## LESSON OUTLINE

**TIME (45 mins)** | **LEARNING EXPERIENCE** | **RESOURCES**

### DO NOW (10 mins)
- **Cultural Connection:** Display image of a tūī bird and ask students to share what they know about this native bird
- **Review:** Quick review of basic sentence structure using examples from previous lessons
- **Engagement:** Show a simple sentence "The tūī flew" and ask students to think about what questions they could ask to add more detail

**Resources:** Tūī bird image, whiteboard, sentence examples from previous lessons

### LESSON BODY (50 mins)

**Introduction to Sentence Expansion (10 mins)**
- Explain the concept of sentence expansion using the Writing Revolution methodology
- Demonstrate the question words: Who? What? When? Where? Why? How?
- Show cultural example: "The kaumātua spoke" → "The respected kaumātua spoke about whakapapa during the hui"
- Discuss how cultural knowledge adds meaning and detail to sentences

**Guided Practice (20 mins)**
- Work through examples together as a class:
  1. Basic: "The waka arrived."
  2. Add WHO: "The ancestral waka arrived."
  3. Add WHERE: "The ancestral waka arrived at the sacred beach."
  4. Add WHEN: "Yesterday, the ancestral waka arrived at the sacred beach."
  5. Add WHY: "Yesterday, the ancestral waka arrived at the sacred beach to celebrate the new year."

**Independent Practice (15 mins)**
- Students work in pairs to expand these kernel sentences:
  - "The kai was prepared."
  - "The karakia was said."
  - "The students learned."
- Each pair must add at least 3 details using the question words
- Encourage use of te reo Māori vocabulary and cultural contexts

**Cultural Integration (5 mins)**
- Share examples of expanded sentences that include cultural knowledge
- Discuss how adding cultural details makes writing more meaningful and authentic
- Connect to the concept of mana and respect in language use

**Resources:** Sentence expansion templates, cultural vocabulary lists, te reo Māori dictionaries, example sentences on cards

### Success Criteria (15 mins)
- **Student Demonstration:** Students share their expanded sentences with the class
- **Formative Assessment:** Teacher observes sentence complexity and cultural integration
- **Cultural Knowledge Application:** Students explain the cultural significance of their added details
- **Next Steps Preparation:** Introduce homework assignment to expand sentences about personal whakapapa

**Resources:** Student work samples, assessment checklist, homework assignment sheet

## "Plan B" (Technology Failure Alternatives)
- **Hands-on Activity:** Use sentence cards and cultural image cards for physical sentence building
- **Cultural Activities:** Oral storytelling circle where students expand sentences verbally
- **Peer Learning:** Small group sentence expansion games using cultural themes
- **Independent Practice:** Worksheet-based sentence expansion with cultural contexts

## Filler Activities (Five minute activities for early finishers)
- **Vocabulary Games:** Te reo Māori word matching games
- **Cultural Knowledge Challenges:** Quick facts about native birds and their cultural significance
- **Reading Practice:** Short passages about Māori cultural practices
- **Writing Prompts:** Quick sentence expansion challenges using cultural themes

## Lesson Reflection
**What worked well:**
- Students engaged enthusiastically with cultural content
- Sentence expansion concept was clearly understood
- Cultural vocabulary integration enhanced learning

**Areas for improvement:**
- Need more scaffolding for students with limited te reo Māori knowledge
- Could include more visual supports for sentence structure
- Should provide more examples of cultural contexts

**Student engagement:**
- High engagement with cultural themes
- Good peer collaboration during practice activities
- Students showed pride in using te reo Māori vocabulary

## Next Steps
- **Follow-up Lesson:** Introduce compound sentences using coordinating conjunctions
- **Homework:** Sentence expansion practice using family and cultural contexts
- **Assessment:** Weekly sentence construction check incorporating cultural knowledge
- **Differentiation:** Provide additional support for students needing more scaffolding

## Cultural Integration Notes
This lesson successfully integrates mātauranga Māori by:
- Using authentic cultural contexts (tūī, waka, kaumātua, karakia)
- Incorporating te reo Māori vocabulary naturally
- Respecting cultural protocols and values
- Connecting to students' cultural identity and knowledge
- Honoring traditional ways of knowing and communicating

## Assessment Opportunities
- **Formative:** Observation of sentence expansion during guided and independent practice
- **Self-assessment:** Students reflect on their use of cultural knowledge in writing
- **Peer feedback:** Students share and comment on each other's expanded sentences
- **Cultural knowledge:** Assessment of appropriate use of te reo Māori and cultural concepts
