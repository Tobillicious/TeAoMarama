## TE MĀTAIAHO - Refreshed NZC Draft Document
**Phase 3 (Years 7-8)**

### Course Description
This unit integrates evidence-based structured literacy approaches with mātauranga Māori, providing systematic instruction in reading, writing, and vocabulary development. Students develop foundational literacy skills through explicit phonics instruction, sentence-level writing using The Writing Revolution methodology, and academic vocabulary acquisition. The unit honors both indigenous ways of knowing and systematic literacy instruction, preparing students for NCEA success while strengthening cultural identity.

### New Zealand Curriculum 2007 - TKI NZC Online

### Achievement Objectives - Learners will:

**Reading:**
- Apply knowledge of sound-letter relationships to decode unfamiliar words
- Use comprehension strategies to understand and respond to texts
- Analyze and interpret texts for meaning and purpose
- Develop vocabulary knowledge and word-solving strategies

**Writing:**
- Construct clear, coherent sentences using varied patterns
- Organize ideas into well-structured paragraphs
- Use appropriate language features for different purposes
- Revise and edit writing for clarity and accuracy

**Vocabulary:**
- Understand and use academic vocabulary across subject areas
- Apply morphological knowledge to decode and understand words
- Connect vocabulary learning to cultural contexts and experiences

## MĀNA ŌRITE MO TE MĀTAURANGA MĀORI
- **Tikanga:** The Māori way of doing things - integrating cultural protocols into literacy learning
- **Te Reo Māori:** The Māori way of saying things - incorporating te reo vocabulary and structures
- **Mātauranga Māori:** The Māori way of knowing things - using traditional knowledge as content
- **Ako:** The Māori way of learning - collaborative, reciprocal learning approaches

## Content Integration Summary

This unit plan integrates all the unstaged content from:

1. **Year8WritingRevolution.tsx** - The Writing Revolution methodology with sentence patterns, writing activities, and cultural applications
2. **StructuredLiteracyUnit.tsx** - Systematic phonics instruction using Liz Kane's scope and sequence
3. **Year8ReadingStrategies.tsx** - Comprehensive reading comprehension strategies with cultural contexts
4. **Year8AcademicVocab.tsx** - Academic vocabulary development across subject areas
5. **AdvancedMorphology.tsx** - Morphological analysis and word building skills
6. **PhoneticsCards.tsx** - Phonetic awareness and sound-symbol relationships
7. **PhonologicalAwareness.tsx** - Phonological processing skills
8. **SightWords.tsx** - High-frequency word recognition and fluency

The unit provides a comprehensive, culturally-responsive approach to structured literacy instruction that honors both evidence-based practices and mātauranga Māori, preparing Year 8 students for academic success while strengthening their cultural identity and connection to their heritage.
