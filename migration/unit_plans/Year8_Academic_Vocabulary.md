
# Year 8 Academic Vocabulary Resource

This document provides a list of key academic vocabulary words for Year 8 students. The definitions are accompanied by example sentences that connect to mātauranga Māori and other relevant cultural contexts, in line with the curriculum's focus on integrated learning.

## Core Academic Vocabulary

### Analyze
- **Definition:** To examine something methodically and in detail, typically in order to explain and interpret it.
- **Example:** We will **analyze** the structure of the wharenui to understand how its carvings tell a story.

### Compare
- **Definition:** To note the similarity or dissimilarity between things.
- **Example:** **Compare** the leadership styles of a rangatira and a modern CEO.

### Contrast
- **Definition:** To differ strikingly.
- **Example:** **Contrast** the traditional methods of kai preservation with modern refrigeration.

### Describe
- **Definition:** To give a detailed account in words.
- **Example:** **Describe** the significance of the pōwhiri process to a visitor.

### Evaluate
- **Definition:** To form an idea of the amount, number, or value of; to assess.
- **Example:** **Evaluate** the impact of the Treaty of Waitangi on different iwi.

### Explain
- **Definition:** To make an idea, situation, or problem clear to someone by describing it in more detail or revealing relevant facts or ideas.
- **Example:** **Explain** how the concept of kaitiakitanga influences environmental practices in New Zealand.

### Infer
- **Definition:** To deduce or conclude information from evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements.
- **Example:** From the intricate patterns in the weaving, we can **infer** the importance of storytelling in Māori culture.

### Justify
- **Definition:** To show or prove to be right or reasonable.
- **Example:** **Justify** your choice of materials for building a model waka, considering both tradition and durability.

### Predict
- **Definition:** To say or estimate that a specified thing will happen in the future or will be a consequence of something.
- **Example:** Based on the lunar calendar (maramataka), **predict** the best time for planting kūmara.

### Summarize
- **Definition:** To give a brief statement of the main points of something.
- **Example:** **Summarize** the pūrākau of Rangi and Papa in three sentences.

## Subject-Specific Vocabulary

### Science (Pūtaiao)
- **Hypothesis:** A supposition or proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation.
  - **Example:** Our **hypothesis** is that the water quality in the local awa will be higher in areas with more native plants.
- **Ecosystem:** A biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment.
  - **Example:** The Tāne Mahuta forest is a complex **ecosystem** where every living thing has a role.

### Social Studies (Tikanga-ā-iwi)
- **Perspective:** A particular attitude toward or way of regarding something; a point of view.
  - **Example:** From the **perspective** of the tangata whenua, the new motorway development is a threat to sacred sites.
- **Sovereignty:** The authority of a state to govern itself or another state.
  - **Example:** The debate around **sovereignty** is a central theme in the history of Aotearoa.

### English (Te Reo Pākehā)
- **Metaphor:** A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable.
  - **Example:** The whakataukī "He waka eke noa" is a **metaphor** for our shared journey and collective responsibility.
- **Theme:** The subject of a talk, a piece of writing, a person's thoughts, or an exhibition; a topic.
  - **Example:** The central **theme** of the novel is the search for identity in a multicultural society.
