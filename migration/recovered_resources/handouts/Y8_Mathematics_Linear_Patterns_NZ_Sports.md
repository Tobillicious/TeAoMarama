## 📚 Learning Content

### What Are Linear Patterns?
A linear pattern is a sequence where the same amount is added (or subtracted) each time. When we graph these patterns, they form straight lines. In sports, linear patterns help us understand consistent improvement or decline in performance.

**Key Mathematical Vocabulary**:
- **Pattern/Tauira**: A repeating sequence or trend
- **Linear/Raina**: Forms a straight line when graphed
- **Gradient/Anga**: How steep the line is (rate of change)
- **Y-intercept**: Where the line crosses the y-axis (starting value)

### Linear Patterns in NZ Sports

#### Example 1: All Blacks Try-Scoring Pattern
Let's look at how the All Blacks' tries per game improved over a training season:

| Week | Training Hours | Tries per Game |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| 1    | 10             | 2              |
| 2    | 15             | 3              |
| 3    | 20             | 4              |
| 4    | 25             | 5              |
| 5    | 30             | 6              |

**Pattern Analysis**:
- Training hours increase by 5 each week
- Tries per game increase by 1 each week
- This shows a linear relationship!

**Algebraic Rule**: Tries = 0.2 × Training Hours + 0
**In Function Form**: y = 0.2x (where x = training hours, y = tries per game)

#### Example 2: Rowing Club Membership Growth
The Karapiro Rowing Club tracked their membership growth:

| Year | Members |
|------|---------|
| 2020 | 45      |
| 2021 | 52      |
| 2022 | 59      |
| 2023 | 66      |
| 2024 | 73      |

**Pattern**: +7 members each year
**Rule**: Members = 7 × Years since 2020 + 45
**Function**: y = 7x + 45

### Activity 1: Pattern Detective - Netball Shooting Accuracy
**Instructions**: 
Analyze this data from the Silver Ferns shooting practice:

| Week | Practice Shots | Goals Scored |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| 1    | 20             | 14           |
| 2    | 30             | 19           |
| 3    | 40             | 24           |
| 4    | 50             | 29           |
| 5    | 60             | 34           |

1. Is this a linear pattern? How can you tell?
2. What's the rate of change (how many more goals per 10 extra shots)?
3. Write the algebraic rule connecting practice shots to goals scored
4. Use your rule to predict goals scored with 70 practice shots

**Materials**: Calculator, graph paper
**Extension**: Graph the relationship and identify the gradient and y-intercept

1. Complete the pattern to find runs after 7 and 8 powerplay overs
