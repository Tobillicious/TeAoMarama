# Te Tiriti o Waitangi: Partnership and Power - Year 7
*Understanding New Zealand's founding document through multiple perspectives*

**Success Criteria**:
- I can explain what Te Tiriti o Waitangi promised to different groups

## Rich Text Resource: Multiple Perspectives on Te Tiriti

### 📜 **What Te Tiriti Promised (Te Reo Māori Version)**

**Article 1 (Tino Rangatiratanga)**: 
- Māori chiefs give governance rights to Queen Victoria
- BUT keep their authority over their people and lands

**Article 2 (Taonga)**: 
- Māori keep full authority over their lands, villages, and treasures
- British get pre-emption rights (first option to buy land)

**Article 3 (Ōritetanga)**:
- Māori get full rights and privileges as British subjects
- Same protection under British law

### 📰 **Different Perspectives on Treaty Day - February 6, 1840**

**🏛️ Governor Hobson's Perspective**:
*"Finally, we have brought civilization and British law to these islands. The chiefs have willingly signed away their sovereignty, and now we can establish proper government and commerce."*

**👑 Māori Rangatira Perspective (Chief Kawiti)**:
*"We have agreed to share this land with the Queen's people, but we keep our mana over our iwi and whenua. This partnership will protect our people while allowing trade."*

**⛪ Missionary Perspective (Henry Williams)**:
*"This treaty will bring peace between Māori and settlers. Both peoples can live together under British protection, and we can continue our Christian mission."*

**🚢 Settler Perspective (Early Colonist)**:
*"At last, we have legal ownership and protection. We can now safely invest in farms and businesses, knowing British law will protect our property."*

## Document Analysis Activities

### 📖 **Activity 1: Perspective Detective**
**Time**: 30 minutes | **Materials**: Perspective cards, comparison chart

**Your Task**: 
1. Read each perspective above carefully
2. Identify what each group hoped to gain from Te Tiriti
3. Find where different groups might have different understandings
4. Predict potential future conflicts based on these differences

**Analysis Questions**:
- What did "sovereignty" mean to Hobson vs Māori chiefs?
- How might language translation have caused misunderstandings?
- Which group's expectations seem most realistic? Why?

**🚩 Cultural Note**: Remember these are historical perspectives. Modern understanding includes many more viewpoints.

---

### ⚖️ **Activity 2: Treaty Principles Investigation**
**Time**: 25 minutes | **Materials**: Modern examples, news articles

**The Three Principles** (developed by courts and government):
1. **Partnership**: Government and Māori work together as partners
2. **Participation**: Māori participate actively in government decisions
3. **Protection**: Government protects Māori interests and culture

**Modern Examples to Investigate**:
- **Partnership**: Co-governance of rivers and forests
- **Participation**: Māori seats in Parliament, iwi consultation on major projects
- **Protection**: Te reo Māori in schools, protection of sacred sites

**Your Task**: Find one current news story showing these principles in action (or being debated).

---

### 🗺️ **Activity 3: Then and Now Comparison**
**Time**: 35 minutes | **Materials**: Historical maps, current demographic data

**Compare 1840 vs 2024**:

| Aspect | 1840 | 2024 |
|--------|------|------|
| **Māori Population** | ~70,000-80,000 (majority) | ~892,000 (17.8%) |
| **European Population** | ~2,000 (tiny minority) | ~3.4 million (70.2%) |
| **Land Ownership** | Mostly Māori | Mostly European/Crown |
| **Language** | Te reo dominant | English dominant, te reo reviving |
| **Government** | Iwi-based systems | Westminster system + iwi consultation |

**Discussion Questions**:
- How did these dramatic changes happen?
- What stayed the same? What changed completely?
- How do Treaty principles apply to this modern reality?

## Critical Thinking: Multiple Truths

### 🤔 **Understanding Different Truths**
Te Tiriti o Waitangi shows us that different groups can experience the same event very differently:

**Māori Truth**: "We agreed to share governance while keeping our authority over our people and lands"
**British Truth**: "The chiefs ceded sovereignty to the Crown in exchange for protection and British citizenship"

**Both can be true** because:
- Language barriers created different understandings
- Cultural differences meant words like "sovereignty" had different meanings
- People heard what they hoped/expected to hear
- The document itself had differences between English and te reo versions

### 🌍 **Modern Applications**
This understanding helps us today when:
- Different cultural groups have different perspectives on events
- Historical events are remembered differently by different communities
- We need to find ways for multiple truths to coexist respectfully

## Cultural Connections and Safety

### 🌿 **Respecting Māori Perspectives**
When studying Te Tiriti:
- **Listen first** to tangata whenua perspectives
- **Acknowledge complexity** - there's no simple "right" answer
- **Respect ongoing effects** - Treaty issues aren't just historical
- **Honor multiple voices** - different iwi may have different views

### 🤝 **Building Understanding**
Te Tiriti teaches us about:
- **Partnership**: Working together despite differences
- **Communication**: How important clear, respectful communication is
- **Justice**: Addressing wrongs and building fair relationships
- **Identity**: How we can all belong in Aotearoa New Zealand

## Teacher Notes

### **Cultural Sensitivity Requirements**
- **Essential**: Have local iwi perspectives represented appropriately
- **Consultation**: Check interpretations with Māori colleagues or community members
- **Balance**: Ensure both historical context and ongoing relevance are clear
- **Respectful Language**: Use appropriate te reo terms and correct pronunciations

### **Historical Accuracy**
- Emphasize that our understanding of the Treaty has evolved over time
- Include recent scholarship and Waitangi Tribunal findings
- Acknowledge areas where historical evidence is limited or contested
- Connect to contemporary Treaty jurisprudence and government policy

### **Differentiation Strategies**
- **Support**: Provide vocabulary support and graphic organizers for perspective comparison
- **Extension**: Include analysis of specific Treaty settlements and their outcomes
- **Visual Learners**: Use maps, timelines, and artwork to support understanding
- **ELL Support**: Pre-teach key historical and cultural vocabulary

- **Truth can be multiple** - more than one perspective can be valid
- **Partnership requires work** - building relationships across difference takes effort
