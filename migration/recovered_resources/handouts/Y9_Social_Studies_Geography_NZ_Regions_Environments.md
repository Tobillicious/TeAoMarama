## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Identify and describe the major geographical regions of New Zealand
- Explain how physical and human factors shape regional environments
- Analyze the relationship between people and their environments
- Compare and contrast different NZ regions and their characteristics
- Understand how environmental changes affect communities and cultures

## 🏛️ **Content Structure**

### **1. Introduction to NZ Geography (10 minutes)**
**New Zealand's Physical Setting**:
- Two main islands (North and South) plus smaller islands
- Diverse landscapes: mountains, plains, coasts, forests
- Volcanic activity and geothermal features
- Climate variations from subtropical to subantarctic

**Geographical Regions Overview**:
- Northland, Auckland, Waikato, Bay of Plenty
- Gisborne, Hawke's Bay, Taranaki, Manawatu-Wanganui
- Wellington, Marlborough, Nelson, Westland
- Canterbury, Otago, Southland

### **2. North Island Regions (20 minutes)**
**Northern Regions**:
- **Northland**: Subtropical climate, beaches, kauri forests
- **Auckland**: Largest city, volcanic cones, harbors
- **Waikato**: Fertile plains, dairy farming, Hamilton
- **Bay of Plenty**: Coastal areas, horticulture, tourism

**Central Regions**:
- **Gisborne**: East Coast, first to see sunrise, agriculture
- **Hawke's Bay**: Wine country, art deco architecture
- **Taranaki**: Mountain, dairy farming, oil and gas
- **Manawatu-Wanganui**: River valleys, agriculture, Palmerston North

**Southern North Island**:
- **Wellington**: Capital city, harbor, government, arts

### **3. South Island Regions (20 minutes)**
**Northern South Island**:
- **Marlborough**: Wine country, sounds, maritime history
- **Nelson**: Sunshine capital, arts community, national parks
- **Westland**: West Coast, glaciers, mining history

**Central and Southern**:
- **Canterbury**: Plains, Christchurch, agriculture
- **Otago**: Dunedin, university city, gold mining history
- **Southland**: Invercargill, farming, southernmost region

### **4. Environmental Factors and Human Impact (15 minutes)**
**Physical Factors**:
- **Climate**: Temperature, rainfall, wind patterns
- **Landforms**: Mountains, valleys, coasts, plains
- **Natural Resources**: Minerals, forests, water, soil
- **Natural Hazards**: Earthquakes, volcanoes, floods

**Human Factors**:
- **Settlement Patterns**: Urban vs rural distribution
- **Economic Activities**: Agriculture, tourism, industry
- **Transportation**: Roads, ports, airports
- **Cultural Heritage**: Historical sites, traditions

## 🎮 **Learning Activities**

### **Activity 1: Regional Profile Research**
**Duration**: 25 minutes
**Materials**: NZ maps, research materials, presentation tools

**Instructions**:
1. Students work in groups to research one NZ region
2. Create a regional profile including:
   - Physical characteristics
   - Climate and weather patterns
   - Economic activities
   - Population and settlements
   - Environmental challenges
3. Present findings to the class
4. Compare and contrast different regions

**Research Focus Areas**:
- **Physical Geography**: Landforms, climate, natural features
- **Human Geography**: Population, economy, culture
- **Environmental Issues**: Conservation, sustainability, challenges
- **Future Prospects**: Development, opportunities, concerns

### **Activity 2: Environmental Impact Analysis**
**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Materials**: Case study materials, analysis worksheets

**Instructions**:
1. Analyze environmental changes in NZ regions
2. Identify causes and effects of environmental changes
3. Consider human responses and adaptations
4. Discuss sustainable development strategies
5. Propose solutions for environmental challenges

**Case Study Topics**:
- Climate change impacts on coastal regions
- Agricultural development and land use changes
- Urban growth and environmental pressure
- Conservation efforts and protected areas

### **Activity 3: Regional Comparison Project**
**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Materials**: Comparison charts, research resources

**Instructions**:
1. Choose two contrasting NZ regions
2. Create a comparison chart covering:
   - Physical characteristics
   - Economic activities
   - Population patterns
   - Environmental challenges
3. Identify similarities and differences
4. Explain factors contributing to regional differences
5. Present findings to classmates
