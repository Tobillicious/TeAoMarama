# Year 9 Science: Native Plant Adaptation in Aotearoa Ecosystems  
**Kaupapa (Context):** Exploring how Aotearoa’s native plants (Rākau Māori) have adapted to unique environments.  

## **Whāinga Ako (Learning Objectives)**  
By the end of this lesson, students will:  
1. Identify **3 adaptations** of native Aotearoa plants (e.g., ponga, harakeke, pūriri).  
2. Explain how these adaptations help plants survive in different ecosystems (e.g., ngahere/forest, wetlands).  
3. Connect Māori knowledge (mātauranga Māori) about plant uses (rongoā, weaving, construction).  

---

## **Ngā Taputapu (Resources Needed)**  
- Images/videos of native plants (e.g., kahikatea, mānuka, kōwhai)  
- Real plant samples (if possible)  
- Chart paper & markers  
- Digital devices (for research)  

---

## **Ngā Mahi (Activities)**  

### **1. Whakatau (Engagement – 10 mins)**  
- **Kōrero (Discussion):**  
  - Show images of native plants and ask: *"He aha ēnei rākau? Have you seen these plants before?"*  
  - Discuss: *"Why do you think ponga (silver fern) leaves have a silver underside?"*  

### **2. Rangahau (Investigation – 20 mins)**  
- **Group Research Task:**  
  - Each group researches **one native plant** (e.g., harakeke/flax, pūriri, kawakawa).  
  - Record:  
    - **Physical adaptations** (e.g., thick leaves, deep roots).  
    - **Māori uses** (e.g., harakeke for weaving, kawakawa for rongoā/medicine).  

### **3. Whakawhiti Kōrero (Sharing – 15 mins)**  
- Groups present findings (poster or digital slide).  
- **Te Reo Māori Bonus:** Include **1-2 sentences** in te reo (e.g., *"He rongoā te kawakawa mō te mamae" – Kawakawa is medicine for pain*).  

### **4. Creative Task (10 mins)**  
- **Design a Super Plant!**  
  - Imagine a plant adapted for a future Aotearoa (e.g., drought-resistant).  
  - Draw and label adaptations.

  - Compare native vs. introduced plant adaptations.
