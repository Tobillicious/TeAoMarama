# **Social Studies Year 10 Activity: Economic Systems in Pre-Colonial Aotearoa**  

## **Learning Objectives**  
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:  
1. Describe key features of traditional Māori economic systems.  
2. Compare and contrast pre-colonial Māori economies with modern economic systems.  
3. Understand the role of **tikanga Māori** (customs) and **manaakitanga** (hospitality) in trade and resource management.  
4. Use **Te Reo Māori** terms related to traditional economies (e.g., **taonga, koha, utu, mahinga kai**).  

## **Curriculum Alignment**  
- **NZ Curriculum Level 5 Social Studies**: Understand how economic decisions impact people, communities, and nations.  
- **Te Takanga o Te Wā (Māori History Guidelines)**: Explore Māori economic practices before European contact.  

---

## **Activity 1: Understanding Traditional Māori Economies**  

### **Part A: Research & Discussion (20 mins)**  
Students will:  
1. Read a short text (provided) on **pre-colonial Māori economic systems**, focusing on:  
   - **Mahinga kai** (food gathering/production)  
   - **Koha** (gift exchange) vs. trade  
   - **Utu** (reciprocity/balance) in economic relationships  
   - **Rāhui** (temporary bans to protect resources)  

2. Discuss in pairs:  
   - How was wealth measured in traditional Māori society? (e.g., **taonga** vs. money)  
   - Why was **manaakitanga** important in trade?  

### **Part B: Comparison Activity (15 mins)**  
Students complete a Venn diagram comparing:  
- **Traditional Māori economy** (e.g., communal ownership, barter)  
- **Modern capitalist economy** (e.g., private ownership, money-based)  

**Te Reo Māori prompt**:  
*"He aha ngā huarahi e rua? What are the key differences?"*  

---

## **Activity 2: Role-Play – Trading in a Pre-Colonial Hapū**  

### **Scenario:**  
Students take on roles in a **hapū** (sub-tribe) preparing for a **hākari** (feast). Each group represents a different **iwi** with unique resources (e.g., seafood, pounamu, kūmara).  

**Task:**  
1. Negotiate trades using **koha** (gifts) rather than money.  
2. Discuss how **utu** (balance) ensures fairness.  
3. Reflect: How does this system differ from modern trade?  

**Assessment Criteria (Formative):**  
✅ Participation in discussion  
✅ Accurate use of **Te Reo Māori** terms  
✅ Understanding of **tikanga** in trade  

---

## **Conclusion & Reflection**  

Students write a short reflection (5 sentences) answering:  
- *"How did traditional Māori economies support sustainability and community?"*  
- *"What can modern economies learn from these systems?"*  

**Optional Extension:**  
Research how **Māori businesses today** (e.g., **kaitiakitanga-based tourism**) blend traditional and modern economic practices.

## **Teacher Notes**  
- **Cultural Safety:** Ensure discussions respect Māori perspectives.  
- **Support:** Provide glossaries for **Te Reo Māori** terms.  
- **Assessment:** Use discussion contributions and written reflections for formative feedback.
