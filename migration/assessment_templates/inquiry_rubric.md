# Inquiry Rubric (4 Levels) – Cultural Responsiveness Included

CULTURAL FLAG: All cultural references/examples must be locally reviewed. Engage whānau/iwi for sensitive content.

| Criteria | Emerging (1) | Developing (2) | Proficient (3) | Advanced (4) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Questioning | Questions are unclear or off-topic | Questions identify a topic but lack focus | Questions are clear, focused, and researchable | Questions show insight, multiple perspectives, and refine over time |
| Evidence & Methods | Minimal evidence; unclear methods | Some evidence; methods partially fit | Sufficient valid evidence; methods fit questions | Robust, triangulated evidence; methods justified |
| Reasoning | Conclusions not supported | Some links between evidence and claims | Logical links; considers counter-examples | Sophisticated reasoning; evaluates limitations |
| Communication | Basic description; limited visuals | Clear structure; some visuals | Well-structured; effective visuals and language | Impactful communication; visuals, media, and language tailored to audience |
| Cultural Responsiveness (CULTURAL FLAG) | Ignores local context or protocols | Mentions culture but lacks accuracy or respect | Accurate, respectful inclusion; acknowledges limits | Deeply engaged with local protocols; consulted appropriately; attributes sources |

Teacher notes:
- Use with co-constructed success criteria where possible.
- If inquiry touches on Māori knowledge, activate review and consultation steps.
- Encourage students to attribute sources and reflect on cultural considerations.
