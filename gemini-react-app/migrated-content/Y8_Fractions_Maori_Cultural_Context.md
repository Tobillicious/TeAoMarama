## 📖 Lesson Content

### **Kōrero Timatanga (Opening Discussion)**

*"Kia ora tamariki! Today we explore how our tīpuna (ancestors) understood parts and wholes through their daily lives in te taiao (the natural world)."*

**Traditional Context**: 
Before calculators and textbooks, Māori people used sophisticated mathematical thinking in their daily lives - from dividing kai (food) fairly among whānau, to understanding seasonal cycles, to managing resources sustainably.

---

### **Activity 1: Hangi Preparation - Equal Sharing**

**Scenario**: Your whānau is preparing a hangi for 24 people. You need to divide the kai equally.

**Cultural Learning**: Understanding **manaakitanga** through mathematical thinking.

**Problems**:
1. If you have 12 kūmara (sweet potatoes), what fraction does each person receive?
   - Answer: 12 ÷ 24 = 1/2 kūmara per person

2. Your whānau contributes 1/3 of the meat, another whānau contributes 1/4, and a third contributes 1/6. What fraction is still needed?
   - Solution: 1/3 + 1/4 + 1/6 = 4/12 + 3/12 + 2/12 = 9/12 = 3/4
   - Still needed: 1 - 3/4 = 1/4

**Te Reo Vocabulary**:
- **Hauwhā** = quarter (1/4)
- **Hawhe** = half (1/2)  
- **Hautoru** = third (1/3)
- **Rite** = equal/fair

---

### **Activity 2: Whakapapa Connections - Fraction Families**

**Cultural Learning**: Using **whakapapa** (genealogical connections) to understand fraction relationships.

**Concept**: Just as people have family relationships, fractions have family connections.

**Visual Representation**:
```
        1 (Tipuna - Ancestor)
       / \
      1/2  1/2 (Parents)
     /  \  /  \
   1/4 1/4 1/4 1/4 (Tamariki - Children)
```

**Activity**: Create fraction family trees showing:
- How 1/2 and 1/2 combine to make 1 whole
- How four 1/4 pieces create unity (kotahitanga)
- How equivalent fractions are like extended whānau (2/4 = 1/2)

**Cultural Connection**: "Just as every person has their place in the whānau, every fraction has its place in creating wholeness."

---

### **Activity 3: Seasonal Cycles - Time Fractions**

**Cultural Learning**: Understanding **māramataka** (Māori calendar) through fractions.

**Traditional Knowledge**: Māori divided the year into seasons based on celestial observations and natural indicators.

**Problems**:
1. **Raumati** (summer) lasts approximately 1/4 of the year. How many months is this?
   - 1/4 × 12 months = 3 months

2. If **Ngahuru** (autumn) is 3 months and **Takurua** (winter) is also 3 months, what fraction of the year do they represent together?
   - 3/12 + 3/12 = 6/12 = 1/2 of the year

**Cultural Insight**: "Our tīpuna understood that all seasons are necessary for the cycle of life - each fraction of time has its purpose."

---

### **Activity 4: Waka Building - Measurement Fractions**

**Cultural Learning**: Traditional boat building requires precise mathematical understanding.

**Scenario**: Building a waka requires careful measurement and proportion.

**Problems**:
1. If the waka hull is 12 meters long, and the taurapa (stern post) should be 1/8 of the total length, how long should it be?
   - 1/8 × 12 = 1.5 meters

2. The sail covers 3/5 of the mast height. If the mast is 10 meters tall, what's the sail height?
   - 3/5 × 10 = 6 meters

**Cultural Connection**: "Precision in measurement ensures the waka will sail safely - mathematics serves our people's wellbeing."
