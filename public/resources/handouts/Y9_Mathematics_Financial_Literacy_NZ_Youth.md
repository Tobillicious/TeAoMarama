### 📊 **Banking and Savings Mathematics**

**Simple Interest Calculations**:
**Formula**: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time (I = PRT)

**Example - Holiday Job Savings**:
Aroha works 20 hours/week during summer holidays (8 weeks) at $20/hour.

- **Gross earnings**: 20 × 8 × $20 = $3,200
- **Tax (10.5% for income under $14,000)**: $3,200 × 0.105 = $336
- **Net earnings**: $3,200 - $336 = $2,864

She puts $2,000 into a savings account earning 4.5% per annum.

- **Interest after 1 year**: $2,000 × 0.045 × 1 = $90
- **Total after 1 year**: $2,000 + $90 = $2,090

**Compound Interest - The Power of Early Saving**:
**Formula**: A = P(1 + r)^t

**Scenario**: Two friends start saving at different ages:

- **Emma starts at 16**: Saves $50/month for 2 years, then stops
- **Jake starts at 18**: Saves $50/month for 40 years

*Emma's savings (compounded at 6% annually)*:

- **Amount after 2 years**: $50 × 24 = $1,200 invested
- **Value at age 65 (47 years of compound growth)**: $1,200 × (1.06)^47 = $19,129

*Jake's savings*:

- **Total invested**: $50 × 12 × 40 = $24,000
- **Value at age 65**: Would need complex annuity calculation, but approximately $77,000

**Key Insight**: Emma invests only $1,200 but benefits from 47 years of compound growth, while Jake invests $24,000 over 40 years. Starting early is powerful!

### 🏠 **Housing and Rent Mathematics**

**Current New Zealand Housing Market (2024)**:

- **Median house price**: $820,000 (national average)
- **Auckland median**: $1,150,000
- **Wellington median**: $850,000
- **Christchurch median**: $650,000
- **Regional medians**: $450,000-$700,000

**Rental Market Analysis**:

- **Auckland average rent**: $650/week (1-2 bedroom apartment)
- **Wellington average**: $550/week
- **Christchurch average**: $420/week
- **Regional averages**: $350-$450/week

**Affordability Calculations**:
**30% Rule**: Housing costs should not exceed 30% of gross income

**Example**: Jordan earns $55,000/year gross income

- **Monthly gross income**: $55,000 ÷ 12 = $4,583
- **Maximum housing budget**: $4,583 × 0.30 = $1,375/month
- **Maximum weekly rent**: $1,375 ÷ 4.33 = $317/week

**Shared Accommodation Mathematics**:
If Jordan shares a $600/week rental with 2 flatmates:

- **Jordan's share**: $600 ÷ 3 = $200/week = $867/month
- **Percentage of income**: $867 ÷ $4,583 = 18.9% (well within 30% guideline)

**First Home Buyer Calculations**:
For a $650,000 house with 20% deposit:

- **Deposit required**: $650,000 × 0.20 = $130,000
- **Loan amount**: $650,000 - $130,000 = $520,000
- **Monthly payment (6% interest, 30 years)**: $3,118
- **Annual income needed**: $3,118 × 12 ÷ 0.30 = $124,720

### 💳 **Credit and Debt Mathematics**

**Credit Card Mathematics**:
**New Zealand Credit Card Statistics**:

- **Average interest rate**: 19.95% per annum
- **Average debt per cardholder**: $3,200
- **Minimum payment**: Usually 2-3% of balance

**Debt Trap Example**:
$2,000 credit card debt at 19.95% interest, paying minimum 2.5%:

- **Monthly minimum payment**: $2,000 × 0.025 = $50
- **Monthly interest charge**: $2,000 × (0.1995 ÷ 12) = $33.25
- **Principal reduction**: $50 - $33.25 = $16.75
- **Time to pay off**: Over 20 years!
- **Total paid**: Approximately $8,000

**Smart Repayment Strategy**:
Paying $100/month instead of $50 minimum:

- **Time to pay off**: 2 years
- **Total paid**: $2,400
- **Savings**: $5,600

### 📊 **Activity 2: Financial Decision Making - "The Big Purchase"**

**Time**: 90 minutes | **Materials**: Loan calculators, comparison worksheets, financial scenario data

**Target**: $15,000 for one-year working holiday (UK/Europe)
**Financing Options**:

1. **Savings plan**: Save over 2 years before going
2. **Travel loan**: 11.95% interest, 5-year repayment
3. **Work and save**: Work overseas to fund extended travel
4. **Family loan**: 0% interest with family agreement

**Phase 2 - Mathematical Analysis** (35 minutes):

**Loan Calculation Formulas**:

**Monthly Payment Formula**:
M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]
Where: M = monthly payment, P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, n = number of payments

**Total Interest Formula**:
Total Interest = (Monthly Payment × Number of Payments) - Principal

**Car Purchase Analysis Example**:
$12,000 car with different financing:

**Option 1 - Bank Loan (8.95%, 5 years)**:

- **Monthly interest rate**: 8.95% ÷ 12 = 0.007458
- **Number of payments**: 5 × 12 = 60
- **Monthly payment**: $12,000 × [0.007458(1.007458)^60] / [(1.007458)^60 - 1] = $248.32
- **Total paid**: $248.32 × 60 = $14,899
- **Total interest**: $14,899 - $12,000 = $2,899

**Option 2 - Dealer Finance (12.9%, 6 years)**:

- **Monthly payment**: $211.45
- **Total paid**: $211.45 × 72 = $15,224
- **Total interest**: $3,224

**Option 3 - Hire Purchase (15.9%, 7 years)**:

- **Monthly payment**: $192.18
- **Total paid**: $192.18 × 84 = $16,143
- **Total interest**: $4,143

**Comparison Analysis**:

| Option | Monthly Payment | Total Interest | Pros | Cons |
|--------|----------------|----------------|------|------|
| Bank loan | $248.32 | $2,899 | Lowest total cost | Highest monthly payment |
| Dealer finance | $211.45 | $3,224 | Moderate payments | More total interest |
| Hire purchase | $192.18 | $4,143 | Lowest monthly payment | Highest total cost |

**Phase 3 - Opportunity Cost Analysis** (20 minutes):

**Opportunity Cost Calculations**:
What else could you do with the money?

**Investment Alternative**:
If monthly car payment ($248.32) was invested at 6% annual return:

- **After 5 years**: $17,665
- **Opportunity cost**: $17,665 vs. owning a depreciating car

**Alternative Transport Costs**:

- **Public transport**: $70/month = $4,200 over 5 years

| Financial flexibility | 4 | | | | |

| **Weighted Total** | | | | | |

**Phase 4 - Presentation and Justification** (15 minutes):

**Group Presentations** (3 minutes each):

**Phase 2 - Savings Strategy Mathematical Modeling** (30 minutes):

**Compound Savings Calculations**:

**Scenario A - Conservative Savings (2% interest)**:
Monthly deposit savings with bank account earning 2% annually:
**Formula**: FV = PMT × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r]

**Example**: Saving $500/month for 10 years at 2% annual interest:

- **Monthly interest rate**: 2% ÷ 12 = 0.001667
- **Number of payments**: 10 × 12 = 120
- **Future value**: $500 × [((1.001667)^120 - 1) / 0.001667] = $66,172

**Scenario B - KiwiSaver Growth Fund (6% average return)**:
Same $500/month with higher expected returns:

- **Future value**: $500 × [((1.005)^120 - 1) / 0.005] = $82,007

**Scenario C - Mixed Investment Strategy**:

- **Term deposits**: $200/month at 4% return
- **KiwiSaver**: $300/month at 6% return
- **Combined total**: Calculate each separately and add

**Time to Goal Calculations**:
**Target**: $130,000 house deposit

**Different monthly savings amounts**:

- **$300/month at 6%**: 24.8 years
- **$500/month at 6%**: 13.7 years
- **$800/month at 6%**: 8.2 years
- **$1,200/month at 6%**: 5.4 years

**Income Requirements for Savings Goals**:
To save $800/month, assuming 20% savings rate:

- **Required gross income**: $800 ÷ 0.20 = $4,000/month
- **Annual income needed**: $48,000/year
