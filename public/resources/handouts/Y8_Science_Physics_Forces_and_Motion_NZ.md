## 🏛️ **Content Structure**

### **1. Introduction to Forces (10 minutes)**
**What are Forces?**
- Push and pull actions that can change an object's motion
- Forces are measured in Newtons (N)
- Forces can be contact or non-contact forces

**Everyday NZ Examples**:
- Wind pushing against trees during storms
- Gravity pulling objects toward Earth
- Friction between shoes and pavement
- Magnetic forces in compass navigation

### **2. Types of Forces (15 minutes)**
**Contact Forces**:
- **Friction**: Resistance between surfaces (shoes on pavement, car tires on road)
- **Air Resistance**: Wind resistance on moving objects (parachutes, cyclists)
- **Tension**: Pulling forces in ropes and cables (sailing, rock climbing)
- **Normal Force**: Support force from surfaces (standing on ground, sitting on chair)

**Non-Contact Forces**:
- **Gravity**: Earth's gravitational pull (falling objects, weight)
- **Magnetic Force**: Attraction/repulsion between magnets
- **Electrostatic Force**: Static electricity effects

### **3. Forces and Motion (20 minutes)**
**Newton's Laws in NZ Context**:
- **First Law**: Objects stay at rest or in motion unless acted upon by a force
  - NZ Example: Rugby ball stays in motion until caught or hits ground
- **Second Law**: Force = mass × acceleration
  - NZ Example: Heavier objects need more force to move (logs, rocks)
- **Third Law**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction
  - NZ Example: Paddling a waka (canoe) pushes water backward, boat moves forward

**Balanced vs Unbalanced Forces**:
- **Balanced**: Equal forces in opposite directions (stationary objects)
- **Unbalanced**: Unequal forces causing motion or change

### **4. Practical Applications (15 minutes)**
**NZ Sports and Forces**:
- Rugby: Tackling forces, ball trajectory
- Sailing: Wind forces, water resistance
- Rock climbing: Tension forces, friction
- Cycling: Air resistance, rolling friction

**Transport and Forces**:
- Cars: Engine force vs friction and air resistance
- Trains: Magnetic levitation, friction reduction
- Boats: Water resistance, wind forces
- Aircraft: Lift, thrust, drag, weight

## 🎮 **Learning Activities**

### **Activity 1: Force Investigation Station**
**Duration**: 25 minutes
**Materials**: Various objects, spring scales, ramps, magnets, string

**Instructions**:
1. Set up 4 investigation stations around the classroom
2. Students rotate through stations in groups
3. Each station focuses on a different type of force
4. Record observations and measurements
5. Share findings with the class

**Station Topics**:
- **Station 1**: Friction investigation (different surfaces)
- **Station 2**: Magnetic forces (attraction/repulsion)
- **Station 3**: Gravity and weight measurement
- **Station 4**: Air resistance (falling objects)

### **Activity 2: NZ Sports Force Analysis**
**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Materials**: Sports equipment, video clips, analysis sheets

**Instructions**:
1. Watch short clips of NZ sports (rugby, sailing, cycling)
2. Identify forces acting in each sport
3. Draw force diagrams for key moments
4. Explain how forces affect performance
5. Discuss how athletes use forces to their advantage

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Connect physics to familiar contexts
- Develop observation and analysis skills
- Understand force interactions in motion
- Apply scientific thinking to sports

### **Activity 3: Force Challenge Design**
**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Materials**: Design materials, testing equipment

**Instructions**:
1. Design a simple device that uses forces
2. Examples: paper airplane, balloon rocket, simple machine
3. Test the device and observe forces in action
4. Explain the forces involved
5. Suggest improvements based on force understanding
