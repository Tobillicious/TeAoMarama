# Year 9 Science: Native Plant Adaptation in Aotearoa Ecosystems  
**Kaupapa:** Understanding how native plants survive and thrive in Aotearoa’s unique environments  

---
## **Whāinga Ako (Learning Objectives)**  
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:  
1. Explain how native plants adapt to different ecosystems in Aotearoa (e.g., ngahere/forest, wetlands, coastal areas).  
2. Identify key adaptations of **taonga species** (e.g., harakeke/flax, ponga/silver fern, pūriri).  
3. Understand the connection between Māori knowledge (**mātauranga Māori**) and scientific observations of plant survival.  
4. Investigate how human actions and climate change impact native plant adaptations.  

---
## **Ngā Taputapu (Resources Needed)**  
- Images/videos of native Aotearoa plants (e.g., kōwhai, mānuka, rimu)  
- Samples of leaves/bark (if available)  
- Access to outdoor space (school garden/local ngahere)  
- Student worksheet (adaptation table)  

---
## **Ngā Mahi (Activities)**  

### **1. Whakatau (Engagement – 10 mins)**  
- **Kōrero:** Discuss the concept of **taonga species** – plants valued by Māori for rongoā (medicine), weaving, and food.  
- **Question:** *"He aha ngā āhuatanga o te ngahere e tino rite ai ēnei rākau ki te noho?"* (What forest conditions help these plants survive?)  

### **2. Investigation – Plant Adaptations (20 mins)**  
**Task:** In small groups, students examine images/real samples and complete a table:  

| Rākau (Plant) | Āhuatanga (Adaptation) | Take (Purpose) |  
|---------------|------------------------|----------------|  
| Harakeke      | Thick, fibrous leaves  | Stores water, resists wind |  
| Ponga         | Silver underside       | Reflects light in dark forest |  

**Extension:** Compare exotic vs. native plant adaptations (e.g., pine trees vs. tōtara).  

### **3. Mātauranga Māori Connection (15 mins)**  
- Watch a short video (e.g., DOC’s *"Te Ao Tūroa"*) on traditional uses of plants.  
- **Discussion:** How did Māori knowledge predict scientific understanding of adaptations? (e.g., harakeke’s strength for weaving).  

### **4. Field Study (If possible – 20 mins)**  
- Explore the school grounds/local park to identify adaptations in real plants.  
- Sketch and label 1–2 plants using **Te Reo Māori** terms (e.g., *rau* = leaf, *kākano* = seed).  

---
## **Aromatawai (Assessment)**  
- **Formative:** Group table completion and participation in kōrero.  
- **Summative:** Short written reflection (e.g., *"Explain one adaptation and why it’s important to the ecosystem"*).  

**Success Criteria:**  
- Uses scientific vocabulary (e.g., "structural adaptation").  
- Includes Te Reo Māori terms correctly.  
- Links observations to Māori perspectives.  

---
## **Whakamutunga (Conclusion)**  
- **Waiata:** Sing the first verse of *"Tōia Mai"* (a waiata about teamwork, like ecosystems).  
- **Whakataukī:** *"Ko te whenua te waiū mō ngā uri whakatipu"* (The land provides for future generations).  

**Homework/Extension:** Research a native plant used in rongoā and present 3 facts to the class.  

---
## **Cultural Safety Notes**  
- All plant examples are culturally significant; avoid discussing sacred species (e.g., kauri) without iwi input.  
- Encourage students to share whānau knowledge respectfully.
