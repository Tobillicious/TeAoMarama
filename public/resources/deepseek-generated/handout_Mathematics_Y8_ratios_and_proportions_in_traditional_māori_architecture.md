## **Ngā Tikanga me ngā Kupu / Key Concepts & Vocabulary**  
- **Ratio (Ōwehenga)** – A comparison of two quantities (e.g., height to width).  
- **Proportion (Whakataurite)** – How parts relate to the whole.  
- **Whare (Traditional House)** – Built using precise ratios for strength and symbolism.  
- **Tikanga (Cultural Principles)** – Design respects balance (mana) and environment.  

---

## **Te Tirohanga / Cultural Context**  
Māori architects (tohunga whakairo) used ratios to create strong, balanced whare.  
- The **ridgepole (tāhuhu)** and **wall posts (poupou)** followed strict proportions.  
- A whare’s length-to-height ratio was often **2:1** for stability.  
- Carvings and spacing followed symmetrical ratios for beauty and meaning.  

---

## **Ngā Mahi / Activities**  

### **1. Whare Ratio Investigation**  
**Task:** Examine a diagram of a whare and:  
- Measure the length and height.  
- Calculate the ratio (e.g., 6m length : 3m height = **2:1**).  
- Compare with classmates—did all whare follow similar ratios?  

### **2. Design Your Own Whare**  
**Task:** Sketch a whare using given ratios:  
- Length : Width = **3:1**  
- Ridgepole height : Wall height = **1.5:1**  
- Explain why these ratios ensure stability.  

### **3. Te Reo Māori Problem**  
"E 4m te roa o te whare, e 2m te tiketike. He aha te ōwehenga o te roa ki te tiketike?"  
*(The whare is 4m long and 2m high. What is the length-to-height ratio?)*
